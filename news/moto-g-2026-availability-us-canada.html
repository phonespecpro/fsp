Moto G (2026) Now Available in the US and Canada On Sale Now

December 12, 2025
By: Shedrack

The $199 Value Proposition

Motorola has officially launched the Moto G (2026) in North America, maintaining its aggressive $199 price point while delivering meaningful upgrades over last year's model. Available now through Motorola's website and select carriers, this budget phone comes with Android 16 pre-installed, a capable 50MP camera system, and unique Pantone-certified colors with a premium vegan leather texture.

Where & When to Buy: Full Availability

The Moto G (2026) became available starting December 11, 2025 in both the United States and Canada. Motorola is taking a staggered approach to distribution, with availability expanding through January 2026.

In Canada, the phone is available starting December 11 at motorola.ca, with wider carrier and retailer availability to follow. The unlocked model is priced at $199.99, though pricing may vary slightly by carrier.

While maintaining the same attractive $199 price, the 2026 model brings several notable improvements over its predecessor, particularly in camera capabilities and storage.

Reviewers note that despite having the same chipset, the 2026 model feels "snappier" in everyday use, likely due to software optimizations with Android 16. Motorola promises two major OS upgrades and three years of bi-monthly security updates, giving users a reasonable support window for a budget device.

Bold Design & Pantone Colors

One of the most distinctive features of this year's Moto G is its unique aesthetic. Motorola has partnered with Pantone to offer two certified color options, both featuring a textured vegan leather back that provides a premium feel unusual for a $200 phone.

Slipstream

A magnetic grey hue that Motorola describes as exuding "quiet strength and stoicism".

Cattleya Orchid

A vibrant pink-purple that "radiates creativity, luxury, and passion". Reviewers have called it "ludicrous" in a good way.

The phone maintains practical features like a water-repellent design (IP52 rating), a side-mounted fingerprint sensor, and - notably for a 2026 device - a 3.5mm headphone jack. The build combines a Gorilla Glass 3 front with the vegan leather back and a plastic frame.

Performance & What Reviewers Are Saying

Early reviews paint a picture of a competent budget device that makes strategic compromises to hit its $199 price, with battery life emerging as a particular strength.

"The Moto G (2026) is a surprisingly great $199 phone... While it retains the same MediaTek Dimensity 6300 SoC and 4GB of RAM, the phone sings along with Motorola's version of Android 16." - Android Central

Key Strengths:

  • Exceptional Battery Life: The 5,200mAh battery consistently delivers "two days of worry-free power" in real-world use, with PCMag's video rundown test showing over 16.5 hours of playback.
  • Competent Main Camera: The 50MP main camera, enhanced with Quad Pixel Technology, captures "surprisingly decent pictures" in good light, while the upgraded 32MP front camera improves selfies.
  • Smooth Display Experience: The 120Hz refresh rate makes scrolling and navigation fluid, though the 720p resolution is a noticeable compromise.

Notable Compromises:

  • Low-Resolution Display: The 720p HD+ resolution (262 PPI) results in "noticeably pixelated" text and less sharp visuals compared to 1080p competitors.
  • Limited Performance Headroom: With 4GB of RAM and the Dimensity 6300, the phone can struggle with heavier multitasking, with some reviewers noting "choppiness" in social media scrolling.
  • Basic Audio Quality: While loud enough, the stereo speakers produce "thin-sounding" and "hollow" audio according to detailed tests.

Who Should Consider the Moto G (2026)?

The Moto G (2026) succeeds as a purpose-built budget device for specific user profiles. It's particularly well-suited for:

  • First-time Smartphone Buyers or Students: The low $199 entry price, combined with solid basics and fun color options, makes it an accessible choice.
  • Secondary/Backup Phone Users: Its exceptional battery life and expandable storage (via microSD) make it reliable for travel or as a spare device.
  • Budget-Conscious Media Consumers: The large 6.7" screen and 120Hz refresh rate provide a good viewing experience for streaming, despite the resolution limitation.

For those willing to stretch their budget slightly or find discounts, alternatives like the Samsung Galaxy A16 5G offer higher-resolution displays and longer software support commitments. However, for the strict $199 price point, the Moto G (2026) delivers a compelling package with personality and standout battery life that's now officially on shelves across North America.
