Exclusive Price Leak

Significant pricing details for Vivo's upcoming X300 series have surfaced online just weeks before its scheduled India debut on December 2, 2025. According to reliable tipster Sanju Choudhary, the standard Vivo X300 is expected to start at an attractive price point that positions it directly against competitors like the recently launched OnePlus 15.

In addition to smartphone pricing, the leak also reveals the cost of Vivo's innovative Telephoto Extender Kit, marking one of the first times such detailed accessory pricing has emerged ahead of an official launch in the Indian market.

Color Options & Availability

The leak also suggests that Vivo might introduce an India-exclusive Summit Red color variant for the X300 model, adding to the global color options of Mist Blue and Phantom Black. The X300 Pro, on the other hand, is expected to arrive in Dune Brown and Phantom Black finishes.

Both smartphones are confirmed to be powered by MediaTek's flagship 3nm Dimensity 9500 chipset, paired with the VS1 Pro Imaging chip and V3+ Imaging chip for enhanced photography capabilities. The devices will ship with Android 16-based OriginOS 6 out of the box.

Zeiss Teleconverter Kit Features

The leaked Telephoto Extender Kit, priced at ₹20,999, represents a significant innovation in smartphone photography accessories. This Zeiss-co-engineered 2.35x teleconverter lens provides optical magnification that maintains image quality, unlike digital zoom which typically compromises clarity.

Market Positioning & Competition

At the leaked starting price of ₹74,999 for the base variant, the Vivo X300 positions itself as a strong competitor in the premium smartphone segment. This pricing strategy places it in direct competition with the recently launched OnePlus 15 and other flagships in the sub-₹80,000 price bracket.

The inclusion of the Telephoto Extender Kit as an optional accessory at ₹20,999 provides photography enthusiasts with professional-grade zoom capabilities without the need for carrying additional dedicated camera equipment. This innovative approach could set a new trend in the smartphone industry for modular photography accessories.

What to Expect

The leaked pricing details for the Vivo X300 series and its innovative Telephoto Extender Kit have generated significant excitement ahead of the December 2 India launch. With competitive pricing starting at ₹74,999 for the X300 and ₹20,999 for the teleconverter accessory, Vivo appears to be targeting both photography enthusiasts and general premium smartphone users.

As we approach the official launch date, more details about availability, exact specifications for the Indian market, and potential introductory offers are expected to be revealed. The X300 series, with its flagship MediaTek Dimensity 9500 chipset and innovative photography capabilities, could potentially reshape the premium smartphone landscape in India.
