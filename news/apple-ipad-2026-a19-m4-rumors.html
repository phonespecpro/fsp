EXCLUSIVE LEAK Internal Apple Code Reveals 2026 Plans

Apple’s Next iPad and iPad Air Rumoured to Pack A19 and M4 Chips Ahead of Launch

December 11, 2025
By: Shedrack

The Big Story

In a significant leak, internal Apple code has revealed the company's 2026 strategy for its iPad lineup. The most shocking revelation? The next-generation budget iPad is expected to break with years of tradition and feature the current-generation A19 chip—the same processor found in the iPhone 17. Meanwhile, the iPad Air is set to receive the M4 chip, solidifying its position as a performance powerhouse. This information comes from a pre-release build of iOS 26 examined by Macworld .

The Chip Revolution: A19 & M4 Detailed

Apple A19 Chip

For iPad 12th Gen

Current Device: iPhone 17 Series

Previous iPad Chip: A16 (3-year old architecture)

Performance Gain: ~50% faster than A16

Key Upgrade: 8GB RAM (vs. 6GB in A16) enables Apple Intelligence

Why It Matters:

This marks the first time since the iPad 4 that the budget iPad will get a current-generation iPhone chip, ending the practice of using 2-3 year old silicon . It finally brings full Apple Intelligence (AI) capabilities to the most affordable tablet in Apple's lineup.

Apple M4 Chip

For iPad Air 6th Gen

Current Device: High-end Macs, iPad Pro (2024)

Previous iPad Air Chip: M3

Performance Gain: Expected 10-20% CPU/GPU boost

Key Feature: Continued pro-level performance in a thinner form factor

Why It Matters:

The M4 maintains the iPad Air's position as a near-Pro device. While not a massive leap from the M3, it ensures the Air stays firmly ahead of the base iPad and keeps pace with demanding creative and productivity workflows.

Source of the Leak: Internal Apple Code

Source Internal Apple code document within a pre-release build of iOS 26
iPad 12 Codenames J581 and J582
iPad Air Codenames J707, J708, J737, J738
Key New Component Apple N1 Wireless Chip for Wi-Fi 7 and Bluetooth 6 support
Expected Launch Spring 2026 (March - June timeframe)

Note on Conflicting Reports: Some earlier rumors, including from MacRumors, suggested the iPad 12 would use the A18 chip . This new leak from Macworld, citing direct Apple code, points strongly to the more significant A19 upgrade, though plans could theoretically change before launch.

What This Means for You

For Budget Buyers & Students

This is huge news. The budget iPad will no longer be a "compromise" on core processing power and AI features. If you need Apple Intelligence for school or work but are on a tight budget, waiting for the 2026 model is highly advisable.

For Creative Pros & Power Users

The M4 iPad Air continues to be the sweet spot. If you own an M1 or M2 iPad Air, the M4 offers a decent bump. If you have an M3 model, the upgrade is likely minor unless you specifically need Wi-Fi 7.

For Current iPad 11 Owners

The jump from the A16 to the A19 is substantial (~50% faster, plus Apple Intelligence). This upgrade cycle is more significant than usual, making a 2-year upgrade particularly compelling for tech enthusiasts.

The Rest of the 2026 iPad Lineup

The leaked code reportedly made no mention of the iPad Pro or iPad mini for 2026, suggesting these models may be on hold until 2027 . However, separate reports indicate a major iPad mini update with an A19 Pro chip and an OLED display is planned for the second half of 2026 . The iPad Pro, having just moved to the M5 chip, is not expected to update again so soon.

The Strategic Big Picture

This leak reveals a clear strategy: Democratizing Apple Intelligence. By putting a current-gen chip (A19) in the budget iPad, Apple is ensuring its entire AI ecosystem has the widest possible user base from the start. This move pressures competitors in the budget tablet space and raises the floor for what's considered an "entry-level" experience.
