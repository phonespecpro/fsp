More Details Leak About Poco M8 Pro — Likely a Rebranded Redmi Note 15 Variant

December 18, 2025
By: Shedrack
Leaked Color Options: Black, Blue, Silver

The Core of the Rumor: A Familiar Strategy

POCO's strategy of rebranding Redmi phones for different markets is well-known, and a massive new leak suggests the upcoming POCO M8 Pro is the next candidate. According to reports from WinFuture.de and multiple tech tipsters, the M8 Pro is essentially a rebadged version of the recently launched Redmi Note 15 Pro+, but with one significant—and potentially disappointing—change in the camera department.

The leak includes detailed specifications and high-resolution design renders, showing a phone with a squircle-shaped rear camera module, a hole-punch display, and a design language that closely mirrors its Redmi sibling. The device has also been spotted on the US FCC certification website, revealing key details like battery capacity and charging speed, adding weight to the rumors.

The Rebrand Hypothesis

Multiple sources, including Gadgets360 and Gizmochina, are reporting that the POCO M8 and M8 Pro are "rebadged" or "re-branded" versions of the Redmi Note 15 and Note 15 Pro+, respectively. This is a common practice for Xiaomi's sub-brands to maximize product reach with minimal R&D cost.

However, the rebranding isn't always a perfect copy-paste. The latest leaks point to a deliberate specification tweak aimed at differentiating the POCO model, primarily in its camera system, which may be a point of contention for potential buyers.

Leaked Specifications: Shared DNA, Key Difference

The Major Camera Downgrade

The most talked-about leak is a potential significant downgrade in the primary camera. While the Redmi Note 15 Pro+ boasts a 200MP main sensor, tipsters claim the POCO M8 Pro will swap this for a 50MP main camera. Some reports confusingly mention the Redmi having a "200MP periscope telephoto," but the consensus is a switch to a lower-resolution primary shooter for the POCO variant.

Shared Specs

Display: 6.83" 1.5K (2772x1080) 120Hz OLED panel.

Chipset: Qualcomm Snapdragon 7s Gen 4.

Battery & Charging: 6,500mAh (or 6,330mAh per FCC) with 100W wired fast charging.

Shared Specs (Cont.)

RAM/Storage: 8GB+256GB, 12GB+256GB, 12GB+512GB variants.

Durability: IP68 dust/water resistance rating.

OS: Android 15 with HyperOS 2 (noted as disappointing by some, as Android 16 is already out).

Key Differences

Main Camera: 50MP on POCO vs. 200MP on Redmi (Expected).

Design Tweaks: Slightly different camera module design and POCO branding.

Market Positioning: Potentially different pricing and regional availability strategies.

Tipsters, including Abhishek Yadav, suggest the phone's launch has been slightly delayed and is now expected in January 2026. The base POCO M8 is also part of this series, rumored to be a rebrand of the standard Redmi Note 15 with a Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 and a 5,520mAh battery.

Pricing & Competition: As a rebranded device in the budget to mid-range segment, the POCO M8 Pro's success will hinge heavily on its price. If it launches significantly cheaper than the Redmi Note 15 Pro+, the camera downgrade might be justifiable for cost-conscious buyers. However, in a fiercely competitive segment, this move could be a double-edged sword.
