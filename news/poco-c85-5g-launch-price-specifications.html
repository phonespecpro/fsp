Poco C85 5G Debuts in India With 6,000mAh Battery and 50MP Camera — Price, Specs

December 9, 2025
By: Shedrack

Official Launch: Targeting the Budget Segment

Poco has officially launched its latest budget-friendly smartphone, the Poco C85 5G, in India today, December 9, 2025. As part of the brand's value-driven C-series, the phone is designed to compete in the sub-₹12,000 segment with a focus on delivering a large battery and a big display. The device shares a striking resemblance to the recently launched Redmi 15C 5G, which it is widely considered to be a rebranded version of.

The phone will be available for purchase exclusively on Flipkart starting December 16, 2025, at 12 noon. The company has also announced attractive introductory pricing and launch offers to sweeten the deal for early buyers.

Price, Launch Offers, and Availability

The Poco C85 5G is launched in three memory variants with introductory prices:

  • 4GB RAM + 128GB storage: Priced at ₹11,999
  • 6GB RAM + 128GB storage: Priced at ₹12,999
  • 8GB RAM + 128GB storage: Priced at ₹13,999 (₹14,499 as per some reports)

Poco has announced significant launch offers that bring down the effective starting price:

  • Instant Bank Discount: A flat ₹1,000 off using HDFC, ICICI, or SBI credit/debit cards. This makes the effective starting price ₹10,999.
  • Exchange Bonus: A ₹1,000 bonus on exchanging eligible old devices.
  • No-Cost EMI: Available for a 3-month tenure.

The smartphone comes in three color options with a dual-tone finish: Mystic Purple, Spring Green, and Power Black.

Key Features and Segment Positioning

Battery Life and Charging

The standout feature of the Poco C85 5G is its massive 6,000mAh battery. Poco claims this is capable of delivering over two days of usage on a single charge. It's supported by 33W fast charging, which can reportedly juice up the phone to 50% in about 28 minutes. A unique addition for the segment is 10W wired reverse charging, allowing the phone to act as a power bank to charge other devices like TWS earbuds.

Display and Performance

The phone boasts the "segment's largest 6.9-inch display" with a smooth 120Hz adaptive refresh rate. It is powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 6300 chipset, which Poco states achieves an AnTuTu score of over 450,000, promising reliable performance for daily tasks and casual gaming. The software promise of two Android upgrades and four years of security updates is a notable commitment for a budget device.

Camera and Design

The photography setup is led by a 50MP AI primary camera. The design features a premium dual-tone finish and a slim 7.99mm profile with a quad-curved back for better ergonomics. The IP64 rating provides a practical layer of protection against dust and water splashes.

Final Thoughts and Competition

The Poco C85 5G enters a fiercely competitive budget 5G segment in India. Its primary selling points are clear: a massive two-day battery, a very large display for media consumption, and a respectable software update promise. The introductory price, combined with bank offers, makes it a compelling proposition for users prioritizing battery life and screen size.

It will directly compete with other recent launches in the ₹10,000-₹15,000 range, such as the Redmi 15C 5G (its apparent sibling), Infinix Hot 50, iQOO Z10 Lite, and Lava Blaze 3. While it shares specs and design with the Redmi 15C 5G, Poco's launch offers might give it a temporary edge for value-seeking buyers. Ultimately, the Poco C85 5G successfully delivers on its core promise of combining a giant battery and a large display at an aggressive starting price.
