Samsung Galaxy A07 5G Geekbench Listing Reveals Core Specifications Ahead of Launch

December 16, 2025
By: Shedrack

Samsung's upcoming budget-friendly 5G smartphone, the Galaxy A07 5G, has made an appearance on the popular Geekbench benchmarking platform, revealing several key specifications ahead of its official announcement . The device, identified by the model number SM-A076B, follows the launch of the 4G variant in October 2025 and is expected to hit the market soon .

The listing provides a concrete look at the phone's core hardware, confirming the chipset it will use and the version of Android it will run, giving us a clear picture of what to expect from Samsung's next entry-level 5G contender .

Geekbench 6 Benchmark Scores

The Galaxy A07 5G (SM-A076B) was put through Geekbench's performance tests, yielding the following results for its computational power .

Single-Core Score
729

Measures performance of a single CPU core

Multi-Core Score
1,878

Measures performance of all CPU cores working together

These scores are consistent with devices powered by the MediaTek Dimensity 6300 chipset, offering a baseline for expected daily and gaming performance .

MediaTek Dimensity 6300 Confirmed

The Geekbench listing details an octa-core processor with a configuration of six cores clocked at 2.00GHz and two performance cores running at 2.40GHz . This core layout and clock speed profile are a definitive match for the MediaTek Dimensity 6300 system-on-chip (SoC) .

This is the same chipset that powered its predecessor, the Galaxy A06 5G, indicating that Samsung is prioritizing 5G connectivity and cost-efficiency in this segment rather than a raw performance leap for this generation . The Dimensity 6300 is built on a 6nm process and includes an integrated Mali-G57 MC2 GPU .

RAM & Software

The Geekbench entry lists the device with 4GB of RAM . It's important to note that this could be the base variant, and Samsung may offer other configurations (like 6GB or 8GB RAM) in different markets at launch .

More notably, the listing confirms the phone will run Android 16 out of the box . It will likely be skinned with Samsung's One UI 8 or possibly One UI 8.5, which would make it one of the most affordable Samsung phones to launch with the latest Android version .

Launch Timeline & Other Hints

The appearance on Geekbench, alongside previous sightings on certification sites like Bluetooth SIG and support pages on Samsung's official websites in multiple regions, strongly suggests an imminent launch .

Most reports speculate the phone could be unveiled later in December 2025 or in early January 2026 . Some industry watchers point to a broader February 2026 launch window for a new wave of Galaxy A series phones, which would include the A07 5G .

What to Expect & How It Compares

While the Geekbench leak gives us the silicon and software story, other specifications remain based on expectations and the profile of its 4G sibling, the Galaxy A07, which launched earlier this year .

Based on the Galaxy A07 4G: We can reasonably expect the A07 5G to share many features with its 4G counterpart, such as a 6.7-inch HD+ display with a 90Hz refresh rate, a 50MP main + 2MP depth rear dual-camera system, an 8MP front camera, a 5,000mAh battery, and an IP54 rating for dust and splash resistance . Samsung also promised six major OS updates and six years of security patches for the A07 4G, a policy that will likely extend to the 5G model .

The primary and most significant upgrade for the A07 5G will, of course, be the addition of 5G connectivity, enabled by the Dimensity 6300 chipset. However, as noted by sources like SamMobile, the use of the same processor as the previous generation means users should not expect a significant boost in CPU or GPU performance . The upgrades are likely to be focused on network capability, software, and potentially other areas like display technology or charging speed.

Final Thoughts

The Geekbench listing for the Samsung Galaxy A07 5G has effectively pulled back the curtain on its most critical specs. The confirmation of the MediaTek Dimensity 6300, 4GB of RAM, and Android 16 paints a clear picture of a device designed to bring 5G connectivity and modern software to the most affordable tier of the market.

While the recycled chipset may disappoint those hoping for a performance jump, it should allow Samsung to price the device competitively. If it retains the commendable update policy, capable camera, and large battery of the 4G model, the Galaxy A07 5G could become a compelling option for budget-conscious buyers looking to step into the 5G era. All official details, including pricing, full specifications, and availability, are expected to be revealed by Samsung very soon.
