Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra's Main Camera Specs Leak: 200MP & Improved Optics

November 18, 2025
PhoneSpec Pro
Exclusive Leak Camera Technology Samsung

Detailed specifications for Samsung's upcoming Galaxy S26 Ultra camera system have surfaced, revealing significant improvements to the flagship's photographic capabilities while maintaining the same 200MP sensor foundation. The leaks point to major optical enhancements that could dramatically improve low-light performance and overall image quality .

According to multiple reliable sources including tipster UniverseIce, Samsung is focusing on optical improvements rather than sensor changes, with the main camera receiving a substantial aperture upgrade that should significantly boost light-gathering capability .

Main Camera: Major Aperture Upgrade

200MP Primary Camera Specifications

Sensor

200MP Samsung ISOCELL HP2 (1/1.3-inch)

Aperture

f/1.4 (up from f/1.7 on S25 Ultra)

Light Increase

Approximately 48% more light capture

Features

OIS, Super QPD AF, Dual Slope Gain, Smart ISO Pro

The switch from f/1.7 to f/1.4 aperture represents one of the most significant main camera upgrades in recent Samsung flagship history. This numerically small change translates to approximately 48% more light reaching the sensor, which should dramatically improve low-light photography and reduce noise in challenging lighting conditions .

Low-Light Performance Boost

The wider f/1.4 aperture allows the S26 Ultra's primary camera to capture significantly more light than its predecessor, resulting in improved low-light photography with less noise and better detail retention. This could give Samsung an edge in nighttime and indoor photography scenarios .

Complete Camera System Overview

While the main camera gets the spotlight, the S26 Ultra's complete camera array appears to be receiving targeted improvements across multiple lenses, maintaining the quad-camera setup that has become standard on Ultra models .

Rear Camera Array

  • Primary: 200MP ISOCELL HP2 with f/1.4 aperture, OIS, and Super QPD phase detection autofocus
  • Ultrawide: 50MP sensor (likely Samsung JN3) with f/1.9 aperture and autofocus
  • Telephoto (3x): 10MP/12MP (sources conflict) with f/2.4 aperture, 3x optical zoom, OIS, and autofocus
  • Periscope Telephoto (5x): 50MP (likely Sony IMX854) with f/2.9 aperture (up from f/3.4), 5x optical zoom, and OIS

Front Camera

The front-facing camera is expected to use a 12MP Sony IMX874 sensor, similar to the S25 series, but may feature a wider field of view (approximately 85 degrees compared to 80 degrees) enabled by a larger camera cutout in the display .

The 3x Telephoto Debate

Conflicting Reports

There's significant disagreement among leakers about the 3x telephoto camera. Some sources claim it will upgrade to a 12MP Samsung S5K3LD sensor, while others suggest it will remain at 10MP with a potentially smaller sensor size (1/3.94" vs 1/3.52") .

This potential downgrade might be related to the S26 Ultra's slimmer 7.9mm thick chassis, as revealed in leaked CAD renderings. However, Samsung may compensate with improved optics, AI processing, and the more powerful Snapdragon 8 Gen 5 Elite and Exynos 2600 chipsets .

Video and Software Enhancements

Beyond hardware improvements, the S26 Ultra is expected to introduce several significant software and video recording upgrades that could enhance the overall camera experience .

APV Video Codec

The Galaxy S26 series will reportedly feature Samsung's new APV (Advanced Professional Video) codec, essentially Samsung's answer to Apple's ProRes. This professional-grade video format promises higher quality video editing with smaller file sizes and AI-accelerated workflows, benefiting content creators and professionals .

New Camera Features

Additional leaks suggest the S26 Ultra may include a 24-megapixel default camera mode, new "adaptive pixel" technology that combines multiple low-res shots into a single high-res image, and a focus speed slider for photos and video. Users may also gain the ability to turn off HDR10+ in favor of standard HDR when recording video .

What This Means for Photographers

For mobile photographers and content creators, the S26 Ultra's camera improvements represent an evolution rather than a revolution. The significant aperture upgrades to both the main and 5x periscope cameras should provide tangible benefits in low-light performance, while the new APV codec offers professional video capabilities .

The focus on optical improvements rather than sensor megapixel counts suggests Samsung is prioritizing real-world image quality over specification sheet bragging rights. With the S26 Ultra expected to debut in February 2026, these camera enhancements could help Samsung maintain its position in the competitive flagship smartphone market .

As with all pre-release information, these specifications should be considered tentative until officially confirmed by Samsung. However, the consistency across multiple leaks suggests the overall camera strategy for the S26 Ultra is taking clear shape with an emphasis on improved light capture and professional video capabilities.
