Xiaomi Confirms December 26 Global Launch Window for 17 Ultra Flagship

December 18, 2025
By: Shedrack
Official Renders Based on Leaks

Breaking News: Launch Date Locked In

Xiaomi has officially confirmed what tech enthusiasts have been anticipating for weeks: the Xiaomi 17 Ultra will make its debut in China on December 26, 2025. The announcement, made via Xiaomi's official Weibo account, confirms that the flagship will arrive "next week," with industry sources pinpointing the exact date.

Perhaps even more exciting for international audiences is the news that a global launch is now virtually guaranteed. The smartphone has already been listed on the US Federal Communications Commission (FCC) website with the model number 2512BPNDAG (where "G" denotes global), as well as the IMEI database. This critical certification is the final major hurdle before a smartphone can be sold in markets like North America and Europe, strongly indicating a global rollout will follow the China debut.

Revolutionary Camera Partnership

Xiaomi didn't just announce a date; it revealed a fundamental upgrade to its legendary partnership with Leica. Moving beyond simple co-development, the two companies have entered a "strategic co-creation" model, collaborating from the earliest design stages.

The first fruit of this deeper alliance will be the industry's first Leica APO-certified telephoto lens on a smartphone. APO (Apochromatic) certification is a prestigious standard in professional photography, signifying a lens that virtually eliminates chromatic aberration (color fringing) for exceptional color accuracy and clarity, especially at long zoom ranges. This promises unprecedented improvements in telephoto and low-light photography.

What to Expect: Flagship Specifications

While Xiaomi will reveal all details on December 26, a flood of credible leaks and certifications paint a clear picture of a powerhouse device designed to compete at the very top of the market.

Performance

Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5: Qualcomm's latest and most powerful 3nm flagship chipset.

HyperOS 3 on Android 16: The latest software confirmed via FCC listing.

Camera System

Triple or Quad Setup: Likely a 50MP main (1-inch type), 50MP ultrawide, and a groundbreaking 200MP periscope telephoto sensor.

Leica APO Telephoto: The star of the show, promising pro-level optical performance.

Battery & Charging

Massive ~7,000mAh Battery: A significant jump from its predecessor for unmatched endurance.

Blazing Charging: Expected to support 100W wired and 80W wireless fast charging.

Connectivity & Build

Global 5G Ready: FCC certified for global networks including 5G.

Premium Build: Expected to feature a flat display, titanium frame, and IP68 rating.

Pricing & Global Availability

Price Expectations & Market Strategy

The Xiaomi 17 Ultra is expected to follow the pricing strategy of its highly successful predecessor. The Xiaomi 15 Ultra launched at:

  • CNY 6,499 (approx. $920 / €840) for the 12GB RAM + 256GB storage variant.
  • CNY 7,799 (approx. $1100 / €1000) for the 16GB RAM + 1TB storage model.

Industry reports suggest the 17 Ultra will be priced similarly, offering cutting-edge technology at a notably competitive price point compared to rivals like the Samsung Galaxy S25 Ultra or iPhone 17 Pro Max.

Global Launch Timeline: While the China launch is set for December 26, the global debut—now confirmed by FCC certification—is expected to follow in the first quarter of 2026, likely around March. Pre-orders in China are rumored to open as early as December 25.

Sources & Verification: This report is based on the official announcement from Xiaomi's Weibo account, certification data from the US FCC and IMEI databases, and corroborated by analysis from leading tech publications including GSM Arena, NotebookCheck, Gadgets360, and Gizmochina.
