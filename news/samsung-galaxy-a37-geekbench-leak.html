The Samsung Galaxy A37 is listed with an octa-core chipset carrying the codename s5e8835, which corresponds to Samsung's in-house Exynos 1480 processor . This represents a significant strategic shift from the previous Galaxy A36 model, which utilized Qualcomm's Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 chipset .

The Exynos 1480 features a 4+4 core architecture with four high-performance cores clocked at 2.75GHz and four efficiency cores running at 2.05GHz . This chipset has previously been featured in the higher-end Galaxy A55, indicating Samsung's intention to uplift the performance positioning of its A-series lineup .

üìÖ Expected Launch Timeline

While Samsung hasn't officially announced the launch date for the Galaxy A37, the device's appearance on Geekbench and Samsung's internal test servers suggests that its launch is approaching . Based on the company's typical product cycle and the previous Galaxy A36 launch in March, we can expect the Galaxy A37 to debut around March 2026 .

The device has been spotted with model number SM-A376B in global markets and SM-A376U for the US variant, with the latter referencing several American carriers including AT&T, Verizon, T-Mobile, and UScellular .

üéØ Strategic Significance

The move to Exynos 1480 in the Galaxy A37 marks an important strategic shift for Samsung's mid-range lineup . This change aligns with Samsung's broader initiative to integrate more in-house chipsets across its product portfolio, potentially offering better cost optimization and performance consistency throughout its device ecosystem.
