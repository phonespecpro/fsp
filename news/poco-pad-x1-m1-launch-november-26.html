POCO Expands into Tablet Market with Two Distinct Models

POCO, the popular Xiaomi sub-brand known for its value-focused smartphones, is making a significant push into the tablet market with the simultaneous launch of two distinct models. The Pad X1 positions itself as a performance-focused device, while the Pad M1 aims to dominate the budget segment with exceptional battery life and display quality .

The launch event is scheduled for November 26 at 16:00 GMT+8 in Bali, Indonesia, where POCO will also unveil its F8 series smartphones. This strategic move allows POCO to offer a complete ecosystem of devices to its growing user base .

POCO Pad X1: Performance Powerhouse

Processor Qualcomm Snapdragon 7+ Gen 3
Display 3.2K resolution, 144Hz refresh rate
RAM/Storage 8GB RAM, 256GB storage (expected higher variants)
Camera Dual rear camera system
Features Dolby Vision, Dolby Atmos, Pogo pins for keyboard
Design "Powerfully Slim" tagline, multiple color options

The Pad X1 is positioned as POCO's premium tablet offering, featuring the powerful Snapdragon 7+ Gen 3 chipset that delivers performance between the Snapdragon 8 Gen 1+ and Snapdragon 8 Gen 2 . This makes it particularly capable for gaming, multimedia consumption, and productivity tasks.

With its 3.2K display running at 144Hz, the X1 promises exceptionally smooth scrolling and vibrant visuals. The device supports both Dolby Vision and Dolby Atmos, creating an immersive entertainment experience. The inclusion of pogo pins suggests POCO will offer official keyboard accessories, positioning the X1 as a potential laptop replacement for light productivity work .

POCO Pad M1: Battery Life Champion

Processor Qualcomm Snapdragon 7s Gen 4
Display 12.1-inch IPS LCD, 2.5K, 120Hz
Battery 12,000mAh with 33W fast charging
RAM/Storage 8GB RAM, 256GB storage, microSD expandable
Cameras 8MP rear and front cameras
Audio Quad speakers with Dolby Atmos

The Pad M1 focuses on delivering exceptional value with its massive 12.1-inch display and enormous 12,000mAh battery, making it ideal for extended media consumption and mobile productivity . The Snapdragon 7s Gen 4 chipset provides capable performance for everyday tasks while maintaining power efficiency.

Weighing approximately 610g and measuring 7.5mm thick, the M1 maintains a portable form factor despite its large display . The tablet is expected to feature an IP54 rating for dust and splash resistance, adding to its durability for daily use .

Rebranding Strategy and Market Position

Industry reports suggest both tablets follow POCO's established strategy of rebranding existing Xiaomi products for global markets. The Pad X1 appears to be a rebranded version of the Xiaomi Pad 7, while the Pad M1 is expected to be based on the Redmi Pad 2 Pro .

This approach allows POCO to quickly expand its product portfolio while leveraging Xiaomi's manufacturing scale and supply chain. The strategy has proven successful for POCO's smartphone business and appears to be extending to their tablet lineup.

Pricing and Availability

This positions the M1 competitively in the budget tablet segment against offerings from Samsung, Lenovo, and other manufacturers.

Both tablets will be available through POCO's official channels and partner retailers following the November 26 launch. The company has already begun teaser campaigns and is likely accepting early registrations through its official website .

Key Features at a Glance

  • Performance Tiering: X1 for premium performance, M1 for battery-focused usage
  • Display Technology: High refresh rate displays on both models (144Hz on X1, 120Hz on M1)
  • Battery Excellence: M1's 12,000mAh battery promises exceptional endurance
  • Audio Quality: Both feature quad speakers with Dolby Atmos support
  • Camera Systems: X1 features dual cameras, M1 focuses on essential 8MP sensors
  • Accessory Support: X1 includes pogo pins for keyboard accessories

Market Impact and Conclusion

The simultaneous launch of two distinct tablets represents POCO's most ambitious expansion beyond smartphones to date. By offering both performance-focused and battery-life-focused models, POCO can target different segments of the growing tablet market with its characteristic value proposition.

With competitive specifications and POCO's aggressive pricing strategy, both the Pad X1 and Pad M1 have the potential to disrupt their respective segments. The November 26 launch will reveal final pricing and availability details that will ultimately determine their market success against established competitors from Apple, Samsung, and Lenovo.
