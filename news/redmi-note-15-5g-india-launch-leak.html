Redmi Note 15 5G Chipset Details Leak Ahead of India Launch, Key Features Expected

December 16, 2025
By: Shedrack
Midnight Black

The Leak: What We Know So Far

Xiaomi has officially started teasing the launch of the Redmi Note 15 5G for the Indian market, confirming several key specifications ahead of its scheduled debut on January 6, 2026. This leak, stemming from official teasers and retailer listings, gives us a nearly complete picture of what could be one of the first major smartphone launches of the new year in the competitive mid-range segment.

The device, which may be branded as the "108 MasterPixel Edition" in India, is shaping up to be a significant update focusing on camera capabilities, battery endurance, and a premium design language. This comes after the series' initial launch in China and select global markets in late 2025.

Confirmed Specifications & Features

Performance: Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 Confirmed

The heart of the Redmi Note 15 5G will be the Qualcomm Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 chipset. This 4nm processor features an octa-core CPU with four Cortex-A78 performance cores (up to 2.4GHz) and four Cortex-A55 efficiency cores (up to 1.8GHz), paired with an Adreno 710 GPU. Xiaomi promises this setup delivers a 30% CPU boost and a 10% GPU boost over its predecessor, along with a commitment of "48-month lag-free performance".

Display & Design: A Premium Feel

The phone is confirmed to sport a 6.77-inch curved AMOLED display with a Full HD+ resolution and a smooth 120Hz refresh rate. The display is expected to feature very high peak brightness. The design teasers suggest a slim profile with a metal frame, contributing to a premium in-hand feel often reserved for higher-priced devices.

Camera: 108MP MasterPixel with OIS

As the "MasterPixel" name implies, the headline feature is the 108MP primary camera. This is a significant point for the Indian variant, as the Chinese model of the Redmi Note 15 uses a 50MP sensor. The camera will support Optical Image Stabilization (OIS) and 4K video recording, a feature that returns to the vanilla Note series after several generations. The rear setup is expected to be complemented by an 8MP ultrawide sensor, while a 20MP camera will handle selfies.

Battery, Charging & Durability

Powering the device will be a substantial 5,520mAh battery, which Xiaomi claims will deliver up to 1.6 days of usage and support a 5-year battery lifespan. It will support 45W wired fast charging. For durability, the global variant will come with an IP65 or IP66 rating for dust and water resistance, a welcome addition for a mid-range phone.

Software, Launch & Expected Price

The device will run on HyperOS 2 based on Android 15. It is confirmed to launch in India on January 6, 2026, and will be available for purchase via Amazon. While official pricing is pending, Xiaomi has indicated it will sit in the sub-₹20,000 segment, making it a direct competitor to phones like the OnePlus Nord CE 5.

It's important to note that the Indian/Global model of the Redmi Note 15 5G differs in several key aspects from the version already launched in China. The table below highlights these variations based on current leaks and official data.

Analysis & What It Means

The leaked specifications position the Redmi Note 15 5G as a formidable contender in the crowded sub-₹20,000 segment. The combination of a capable Snapdragon 6 Gen 3 chipset, a high-resolution 108MP OIS camera, a large battery with fast charging, and a premium 120Hz curved AMOLED display checks nearly all the boxes for a modern mid-ranger.

The focus on camera performance with the "MasterPixel" branding suggests Xiaomi is aiming to win over photography enthusiasts on a budget. The return of 4K video recording to the standard Note model is a notable upgrade that users have been requesting.

However, the choice to offer a slightly smaller battery (5,520mAh vs. 5,800mAh) and a lower IP rating (IP65/66 vs. IP68) compared to the Chinese model shows the strategic trade-offs made for different markets. The final verdict will, as always, depend on the official price announced on January 6th. If Xiaomi can price it aggressively starting around ₹18,000, the Redmi Note 15 5G could very well become the new value-for-money champion in early 2026.
