Honor Confirms Win Series Is Arriving This Month as a Performance-Focused Gaming Flagship

December 16, 2025
By: Shedrack

Honor has officially confirmed the imminent launch of its new Win series of smartphones, with the gaming-focused lineup set to debut in China later this month . This marks a strategic rebranding, as the new Win series will replace the existing Honor GT lineup and is positioned directly at the competitive mobile gaming and esports segment .

Teasing the launch with the slogan "Exceptional Strength, Born to Win," Honor has framed this series as its new "yearly esports phone," signaling a dedicated commitment to high-performance hardware for gamers . The series is expected to launch with two flagship models, both designed to offer sustained performance for long gaming sessions .

What We Know: Honor Win Series At A Glance

Launch Date: Officially confirmed for December 2025 in China. An exact date is pending .
Processor: Expected to feature the latest Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset in the Pro model, with the standard variant possibly using the Snapdragon 8 Elite .
Battery Beast: Rumors point to a massive battery, potentially up to 10,000mAh in one model, supported by 100W fast charging .
Active Cooling: Leaked renders show a cutout for an active cooling fan on the back, a feature aimed at maintaining peak performance .

Design & Expected Display

Leaked renders and a teaser image have given us a clear look at the bold design direction for the Win series . The phone features a prominent, large rectangular camera module on the back that houses a triple-camera setup and a lit-up "Win" logo .

Gamer-Centric Display

Reports suggest a ~6.8-inch flat 1.5K OLED display, preferred by gamers for reduced accidental touches and better grip . A 120Hz refresh rate is also expected for smooth visuals .

Color Options

The phones are expected to launch in multiple colorways. Leaks point to Black, Blue, and Cyan for the series, with other reports mentioning Beige and White for specific models .

The Cooling Factor

The visible fan cutout in the camera module is one of the most talked-about features, designed to dissipate heat during intensive gaming, similar to dedicated gaming phones like the Nubia RedMagic series .

Performance & Hardware Rumors

As a series touted for "exceptional strength," the hardware is expected to be top-tier. The higher-end Honor Win Pro model is tipped to run on the latest Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset, while the standard Honor Win might utilize the previous year's Snapdragon 8 Elite .

The inclusion of an active cooling fan strongly suggests Honor's focus is on sustained performance rather than just peak benchmark numbers, which is crucial for long gaming sessions or resource-intensive tasks .

The 10,000mAh Battery Rumors

Perhaps the most eye-catching rumor is the potential inclusion of a 10,000mAh battery in at least one of the Win series models . If true, this would be one of the largest batteries ever seen in a mainstream smartphone and a huge selling point for gamers who prioritize battery life.

This massive capacity is expected to be paired with 100W wired fast charging support to make topping up the large battery quicker . Other reports have also mentioned a large 8,500mAh battery, so the final capacity remains to be confirmed .

Note: Specifications like battery capacity, chipset variants, and final design are based on pre-launch rumors, teasers, and leaks. Official details will be confirmed by Honor at the launch event.

Rebranding from GT to Win

The launch of the Win series represents a clear rebranding of Honor's performance line. Multiple sources confirm that the expected "Honor GT 2" series is now launching under the new Win series branding . This move establishes "Win" as Honor's dedicated gaming and high-performance sub-brand.

The series is currently confirmed for a China launch this month, with a global release likely to follow at a later date if the series proves successful . With this launch, Honor is making a decisive play for the growing mobile esports and hardcore gaming market.
