A Quiet Appearance With Major Specs

Honor has taken the unusual route of a "soft launch" for its latest mid-range contender, the X8d. The device has appeared for sale, listed as "out of stock," on a major retailer's website in Kyrgyzstan, revealing its complete specifications ahead of any formal announcement.

This quiet listing confirms that the X8d is built around one standout feature: a massive 7,000 mAh battery, which promises multi-day endurance. It pairs this with the global debut of a new Qualcomm processor and a high-resolution camera, making a compelling case for users who prioritize battery life and core performance over 5G connectivity.

Endurance First: The 7,000 mAh Powerhouse

The defining characteristic of the Honor X8d is its enormous battery. At 7,000 mAh, it far exceeds the capacity found in most modern smartphones, targeting users who are tired of daily charging.

Multi-Day Battery Life

To refill this large cell, Honor includes support for 45W wired SuperCharge fast charging. While the listing doesn't specify exact charging times, this power level should allow for reasonably quick top-ups despite the battery's size. The phone's physical dimensions—162.9 x 76.3 x 7.5 mm and a weight of 188 grams—are notably slim and light considering the battery capacity within, suggesting efficient internal design.

Debuting a New 4G-Only Platform

The Honor X8d has the distinction of being the first device in the world to feature Qualcomm's new Snapdragon 6s 4G Gen 2 chipset. This confirms that Qualcomm is still actively developing new 4G LTE platforms for markets and users where 5G is not yet a critical requirement.

The device is listed with 8GB of RAM and a choice of 128GB or 256GB of internal storage. For software, the listing mentions it runs Android 15 with MagicOS 10 on top, though some reports note that MagicOS 10 is typically based on Android 16, leaving some ambiguity about the final OS version.

Note: The retail listing states "Android 15, MagicOS 10," but MagicOS 10 is generally built on Android 16. This may be a typo in the listing, and the final software version will be confirmed upon official launch.

Vibrant Display & Resilient Design

Display at a Glance:

The Honor X8d features a large 6.77-inch AMOLED display with a sharp 1080 x 2392 pixel resolution (~388 PPI). A key quality-of-life feature is the 120Hz refresh rate, which makes scrolling and animations look exceptionally smooth. The display also supports 1.07 billion colors for vibrant visuals.

For durability, the phone carries an IP65 rating, certifying it as dust-tight and protected against low-pressure water jets from any direction, offering good peace of mind against everyday splashes and dust. It will be available in three classic color options: Blue, Gray (or Silver), and Black.

Camera: High-Megapixel Main Sensor

The camera system is led by a 108MP main sensor with an f/1.8 aperture. This high-resolution sensor is paired with Optical Image Stabilization (OIS) to help reduce blur in photos and videos. It's accompanied by a 5MP secondary camera, likely an ultrawide or depth sensor, though its exact function isn't specified in the listing.

For selfies and video calls, there is a 16MP front-facing camera housed in a punch-hole cutout. Both front and rear cameras are capable of recording video at 1080p resolution at 30 frames per second, with gyro-based Electronic Image Stabilization (EIS) support on the main camera.

Availability & Initial Verdict

The Honor X8d is listed as "Coming soon" with an expected release in December 2025. Its appearance on a Kyrgyzstani retail site suggests it will first launch in specific regional markets. The price remains unknown until Honor makes an official announcement.

The Bottom Line: The Honor X8d makes a clear trade-off: it forgoes 5G connectivity and potentially the latest Android version to deliver an unmatched battery life in a surprisingly sleek body. With its new 4G chipset, smooth 120Hz AMOLED display, and capable 108MP camera, it is shaping up to be a strong choice for budget-conscious users in regions where 5G isn't yet essential, but long battery life is a top priority.
