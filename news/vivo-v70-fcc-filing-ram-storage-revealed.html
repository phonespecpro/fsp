Vivo V70 Spotted in FCC Filings, Revealing RAM and Storage Configurations Ahead of Launch

December 17, 2025
By: Shedrack

Concept render based on FCC filings and rumors. The FCC certification for model V2538 confirms key details ahead of the Vivo V70's anticipated launch.

Regulatory Green Light: Vivo V70 Details Surface via FCC

Vivo's upcoming mid-range contender, the Vivo V70, has taken a significant step towards its commercial release by appearing on the website of the US Federal Communications Commission (FCC). The certification, which is mandatory for selling devices in the United States, has revealed several concrete specifications, moving details from the realm of rumor into official documentation.

The device is listed under the model number V2538, which has previously been linked to the Vivo V70 through other certifications like the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) and benchmarking sites. This latest filing not only confirms the device's readiness for a global market but also gives us a clearer picture of what to expect in terms of memory, software, and connectivity.

What the FCC Documents Reveal

The FCC filing acts as a formal technical disclosure. Here are the key confirmed details extracted from the certification documents:

Device Model: Vivo V2538 (Vivo V70)
FCC ID: 2AUCY-V2538
Memory Variant: 12GB RAM + 256GB Storage
Operating System: Android 16
Connectivity: 5G NR, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth, NFC, GNSS
Network Bands (5G): n2, n5, n7, n25, n26, n38, n41, n77, n78

This filing confirms at least one high-end memory configuration for the V70. Earlier Geekbench listings had already pointed to an 8GB RAM option, suggesting Vivo will offer multiple variants at launch. The support for a wide array of 5G bands, including those used in North America (n2, n5, n25, n26), strongly indicates plans for a broad international rollout, not limited to Asia.

Chipset and Expected Performance

While the FCC certification focuses on radio frequency and safety compliance, earlier benchmarks have filled in the performance picture. The Vivo V70 (model V2538) was previously listed on Geekbench, revealing it will be powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 7 Gen 4 processor.

The listing showed an octa-core CPU configuration with a prime core clocked at 2.80GHz and an Adreno 722 GPU. The device scored 1,235 points in single-core and 3,920 points in multi-core tests, indicating solid mid-range performance that should succeed the current Vivo V60.

The Bigger Picture: The Upcoming V70 Series

Leaks suggest the Vivo V70 won't arrive alone. According to tipsters, the company is preparing a four-model series for 2026, introducing new naming conventions.

Rumored Vivo V70 Series Lineup

Based on leaks from tipster Paras Guglani.

Vivo V70 5G Vivo V70 Elite 5G Vivo V70 FE 5G Vivo V70 Lite 5G

Key Change: The series is rumored to have no "Pro" models. Instead, "Elite" and "FE" (Fan Edition) variants will be introduced. Reports indicate the standard V70 and V70 FE are targeted for India, while the Lite and Elite models may have a wider global release.

Rumored Full Specifications at a Glance

Beyond the FCC-confirmed details, the Vivo V70 is the subject of numerous leaks, particularly because it is suspected to be a global rebrand of the Vivo S50 recently launched in China. If this holds true, we can expect the following specifications:

Display

6.59-inch AMOLED
1260x2750 resolution
120Hz refresh rate

Rear Camera

50MP Main (OIS)
50MP Periscope Telephoto (3x zoom, OIS)
8MP Ultrawide

Front Camera

50MP Selfie Camera

Battery & Charging

6,500mAh battery
90W wired fast charging

Note: These specifications are based on leaks and the Chinese Vivo S50 model. They are not confirmed by the FCC and may vary for the global V70 release.

Launch Timeline and Conclusion

Vivo has not announced an official launch date. However, the completion of FCC certification is typically one of the final steps before a market release. Multiple sources point to a launch in February 2026, with India expected to be a key market.

Conclusion: The FCC filing for the Vivo V70 has shifted several specs from rumor to near-certainty. With a confirmed high-memory variant, the latest Android OS, and extensive connectivity, the V70 is shaping up to be a competitive player in the 2026 mid-range segment. Its potential rebranding from the camera-focused S50 series also suggests a strong emphasis on imaging capabilities. All eyes are now on Vivo for the official announcement, expected early next year.
