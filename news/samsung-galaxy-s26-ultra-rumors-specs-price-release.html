Samsung Galaxy S26 Ultra: Rumored Specs, Expected Price, and Release Timeline

November 30, 2025
By: Shedrack

Overview

While Samsung's Galaxy S25 Ultra continues to be a dominant force in the smartphone market, attention is already turning to its successor - the Galaxy S26 Ultra. Expected to launch in early 2026, this flagship promises meaningful upgrades in performance, camera capabilities, and charging technology while maintaining Samsung's premium design philosophy.

Based on leaks from reliable sources and industry reports, we're beginning to see a clear picture of what Samsung has in store for its next ultra-premium smartphone. From a powerful new chipset to faster charging capabilities, here's everything we know so far about the Galaxy S26 Ultra.

Expected Release Timeline

Late January 2026
Expected Galaxy Unpacked Event
Early February 2026
Pre-orders Begin
Mid-February 2026
Wider Market Availability

Samsung has been consistent with its Galaxy S series launch schedule in recent years, typically holding Galaxy Unpacked events in January or February . While some reports suggested potential delays due to lineup reorganization, recent information indicates Samsung has resolved these issues and is targeting a late January 2026 launch with market availability by mid-February .

However, conflicting reports from Korean publications suggest a later February 25 Galaxy Unpacked event, which would push retail availability into March 2026 . Samsung's final decision will likely depend on production timelines and competitive considerations against Apple's iPhone 17 series.

Key Rumored Features & Changes

Performance Boost

The Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset is expected to deliver significant performance improvements. Benchmark leaks suggest potential Geekbench scores of 3,832 (single-core) and 12,208 (multi-core), representing a substantial jump over the S25 Ultra's 3,031 and 9,829 scores respectively . This could make the S26 Ultra one of the most powerful Android devices of 2026.

Camera Refinements

While the main camera may retain the 200MP sensor, rumors suggest a wider f/1.4 aperture that could allow 47% more light for improved low-light photography . The 3x telephoto lens might upgrade from 10MP to 12MP, and a new laser AF sensor could provide faster focusing speeds .

Charging Improvements

After years of conservative charging speeds, Samsung might finally upgrade to 60W wired charging (up from 45W) and 25W wireless charging (up from 15W) . This would significantly reduce charging times and better compete with Chinese manufacturers.

Display & Design

The S26 Ultra might feature a thinner profile (7-8mm vs 8.1mm) with slightly increased dimensions . The display could use new M14 OLED materials and CoE technology for better efficiency and potentially higher brightness up to 3000 nits . A new "Flex Magic" privacy feature might limit visibility from side angles to prevent shoulder surfing .

Expected Pricing & Market Position

The Galaxy S26 Ultra will likely maintain its premium positioning, but there are conflicting reports about potential price changes:

Expected Pricing

  • 256GB Model $1,299 (possibly higher)
  • 512GB Model $1,419 (possibly higher)
  • 1TB Model $1,659 (possibly higher)

While Samsung has maintained consistent pricing for the past two Ultra models, some reports suggest a potential price increase due to higher component costs, particularly if the S26 Ultra uses the Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 chipset globally . However, competitive pressure from Apple's iPhone 17 Pro Max at $1,199 might encourage Samsung to maintain current pricing .

Outlook & Final Thoughts

Based on current rumors, the Galaxy S26 Ultra appears to be an evolutionary rather than revolutionary upgrade. The focus seems to be on refining existing features rather than introducing dramatic changes. The most significant improvements likely include the new Snapdragon chipset, faster charging capabilities, and camera optimizations.

For S25 Ultra owners, the upgrade might not be compelling enough to justify the cost, especially if rumors about minimal camera hardware changes prove accurate . However, for those using older models or coming from different brands, the S26 Ultra could represent the pinnacle of Android smartphone technology in early 2026.

As with all pre-release information, these specifications and features are based on leaks and rumors and could change before the official announcement. We'll continue to update this article as new information emerges in the coming months leading up to the expected early 2026 launch.
