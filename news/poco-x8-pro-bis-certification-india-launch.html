Poco X8 Pro Appears on BIS Certification — India Launch Could Be Around the Corner

December 10, 2025
By: Shedrack

Spotted on BIS India

The Poco X8 Pro has been listed on India's Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) certification website, a key regulatory step that all electronics must clear before launch in the country. This is a strong indicator that its official debut in India is imminent.

Model Number: 2511FPC34I

What the BIS Listing Means

Spotted by tipster Sudhanshu Ambhore, the BIS listing for the Poco X8 Pro carries the model number 2511FPC34I. While the certification itself doesn't reveal specifications, its presence strongly signals that Poco has begun the formal process of bringing the phone to the Indian market. The listing suggests that the launch could be just weeks away, with a January 2026 release being widely rumored.

The Rebrand Strategy

The Poco X8 Pro is widely expected to be a global rebrand of the upcoming Redmi Turbo 5, a model slated for a China-exclusive release. This is a common strategy for Xiaomi's sub-brand, allowing it to offer competitive devices tailored for different markets. The Redmi Turbo 5 is rumored to launch in China in early January 2026, with the global Poco X8 Pro launch likely to follow closely after.

Based on leaks and its likely origin as the Redmi Turbo 5, here's what the Poco X8 Pro is expected to bring to the mid-range segment.

The Poco X8 Pro is positioned as the direct successor to the Poco X7 Pro, which launched in India in January 2025 at a starting price of ₹27,999 for the 8GB+256GB variant. Given the expected upgrades, the X8 Pro is tipped to be priced higher, likely above ₹30,000 in India.

The Iron Man Edition

In a parallel development, Xiaomi has also registered a "Poco X8 Pro Iron Man Edition" (model 2511FPC34G) in the GSMA database. This suggests a potential limited edition variant with Marvel-themed customizations, similar to past collaborations, could also be in the works for launch alongside the standard model.

Analysis: A Potent Mid-Range Contender

If the rumors hold true, the Poco X8 Pro appears to be shaping up as a powerful challenger in the competitive ₹30,000-₹40,000 price segment in India.

  • Performance Focus: The Dimensity 8500 chipset, rumored metal frame, and high-refresh-rate display position it as a strong option for gamers and performance enthusiasts.
  • Battery Leadership: Even with a potentially reduced capacity for global markets, a battery larger than its predecessor's 6550mAh is expected to be a major selling point.
  • Premium Touches: Features like an IP68 rating for dust/water resistance and an ultrasonic in-display fingerprint sensor are typically found in more expensive phones, giving the X8 Pro a premium edge.

As always, it's important to treat these details as credible leaks until Poco makes an official announcement. However, the BIS certification is a concrete step that makes a Q1 2026 launch for the Poco X8 Pro in India highly probable.
