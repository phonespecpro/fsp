OnePlus Turbo Series Teased as Next Big Battery Champion With Segment’s Biggest Cells

December 16, 2025
By: Shedrack

In a move set to redefine expectations for battery life in performance phones, OnePlus has officially confirmed it is working on an all-new smartphone series: the OnePlus Turbo . Announced via Weibo by OnePlus China President Li Jie Louis, the new lineup promises "frighteningly strong" performance, battery life, and gaming capabilities designed to lead its class .

While details remain preliminary, the announcement and subsequent leaks suggest OnePlus is targeting the growing market of mobile gamers and power users who prioritize endurance above all else, potentially with the largest batteries ever seen in the brand's phones .

The Official Word from OnePlus

In his Weibo post celebrating OnePlus's anniversary, President Li Jie Louis stated that the Turbo series will focus on leading in performance, battery life, and gaming within its price range . He confirmed the series is "about to launch," with multiple sources pointing to a 2026 release window .

Notably, the executive's language emphasized raw power and endurance over other features, signaling a clear and focused mission for this new product line .

The Rumored Battery Behemoth

8,000 - 9,000 mAh

The segment's biggest cells, rumored to power the OnePlus Turbo series.

While not officially confirmed, the most explosive rumors surrounding the Turbo series point to truly staggering battery capacities. Multiple leaks and industry tipsters suggest batteries ranging from 8,000mAh to approximately 9,000mAh .

For perspective, the current OnePlus 15 features a 7,300mAh battery already praised for "unbelievable battery life," and the OnePlus Ace 6T holds the current brand record at 8,300mAh . A jump to 9,000mAh would represent a new frontier for mainstream smartphone endurance, potentially enabling multi-day usage even under heavy gaming loads .

Battery & Charging

Capacity: 8,000mAh to ~9,000mAh .
Charging: 100W wired fast charging support .
Feature: Bypass charging for gaming .

Performance

Chipset: Qualcomm Snapdragon 8 Elite Gen 5 (3nm) rumored for top model .
Alternative: A modified Snapdragon 8s Gen 4 also speculated .
RAM/Storage: Up to 8GB RAM & 512GB UFS storage .

Display

Size & Type: ~6.7-inch 1.5K resolution AMOLED panel .
Refresh Rate: High refresh rate up to 165Hz for gaming .
Features: 1 billion colors, 120Hz standard refresh rate also mentioned .

Launch & Market

Launch Window: 2026, with January 2026 specifically tipped .
Initial Market: Currently confirmed only for China .
Competition: Positioned as a rival to the Redmi Turbo series .

Expected Camera & Design Details

Leaked specifications suggest the focus on battery and performance might come with a slightly streamlined camera system compared to flagship models like the OnePlus 15 . Rumors point to a dual rear camera setup featuring a 50MP main sensor with Optical Image Stabilization (OIS) and an 8MP ultrawide sensor . A 16MP front-facing camera is also expected .

In terms of durability, the phone is rumored to feature an IP68 rating for dust and water resistance . It will likely run Android 16 with ColorOS 16 out of the box .

Strategy, Availability & The Big Question

The Turbo series appears to be part of a strategic diversification for OnePlus, allowing it to target the performance-gaming segment with a dedicated lineup . By potentially offering flagship-level chipsets and extreme batteries at a competitive price, it could challenge established gaming phone brands .

The Critical Unknown: While officially confirmed for development, the Turbo series has so far only been announced for the Chinese market . A global release remains uncertain, which could disappoint international OnePlus fans eager for this battery-focused powerhouse .

If the rumors hold true, the OnePlus Turbo series launching in 2026 could become the new benchmark for smartphone battery life, finally delivering the "charge once, use for days" experience that power users and gamers have been demanding . All eyes will now be on OnePlus for more official details, pricing, and clarity on worldwide availability.
