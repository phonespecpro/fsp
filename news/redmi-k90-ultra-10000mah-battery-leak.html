Redmi K90 Ultra Leak Suggests Massive 10,000 mAh Battery in a Slim Body Design

December 16, 2025
By: Shedrack

The smartphone world has been hit with a potentially revolutionary leak. According to noted tipsters and industry insiders, Xiaomi's upcoming Redmi K90 Ultra—the brand's rumored "ultimate" flagship for 2026—might defy conventional design wisdom by packing a colossal 10,000 mAh (10,140 mAh rated) battery into a remarkably slim 7.5mm thick aluminum alloy frame .

If accurate, this specification would represent a monumental leap in battery capacity for a mainstream flagship-grade device, potentially redefining endurance standards for power users and gamers while maintaining a premium, sleek form factor . The leak has generated intense excitement and skepticism in equal measure within the tech community .

The Engineering Marvel: Size vs. Thickness

10,000 mAh
inside a body just 7.5mm thin

This leaked combination challenges a fundamental trade-off in smartphone design. Traditionally, large batteries necessitate thick, heavy bodies. The Redmi K90 Ultra leak suggests Xiaomi's engineers may have achieved a breakthrough in battery energy density or internal layout.

For comparison, the current battery life champion, the Redmi K80 Ultra, houses a 6,000 mAh battery in an 8.9mm body . The rumored K90 Ultra spec represents a ~66% increase in capacity with a ~15% reduction in thickness—a feat that seems almost too good to be true .

The Skeptic's Corner: Is This Even Possible?

The leaked specifications, particularly the 10,000 mAh battery in a 7.5mm frame, are extraordinary and have led many analysts to urge caution . The primary challenge is physics and energy density: current lithium-ion and even emerging silicon-based battery technologies have limits on how much energy can be packed into a given volume without increasing thickness or compromising safety.

Expert Analysis: "While not theoretically impossible, a 10,000 mAh battery in a 7.5mm chassis would require a monumental breakthrough in battery cell chemistry or a revolutionary new internal stacking architecture that has eluded the entire industry," notes a battery technology consultant familiar with Xiaomi's R&D. "It's more plausible that the final capacity is lower, perhaps in the 7,000-8,000 mAh range, which would still be a massive achievement." .

Some theories suggest Xiaomi might be using a new "dual-battery" system with two 5,000 mAh cells arranged in a novel configuration to save space, or a next-generation solid-state or semi-solid-state battery with significantly higher energy density that is finally ready for commercialization .

Leak Source & Expected Launch

The information originates from several known Chinese tipsters on platforms like Weibo, who have a mixed but generally reliable track record with Xiaomi and Redmi leaks . The specifications were reportedly sourced from internal engineering prototypes currently being tested.

If the development is on track, the Redmi K90 Ultra is expected to launch in China in the first half of 2026, possibly as early as March or April . A global launch under a different name (possibly as a POCO F-series phone) could follow later in the year . The device is positioned to compete directly with other "ultra" performance phones and gaming flagships .

Final Verdict: A Potential Game-Changer

The Redmi K90 Ultra leak, if even partially true, points to an incredibly ambitious device. The prospect of a phone that combines extreme battery life (potentially 2-3 days of heavy use), flagship performance, and a premium, slim design addresses the top pain points of modern smartphone users in one package.

However, until Xiaomi makes an official announcement or more concrete evidence surfaces, these specs should be treated as exciting but unconfirmed rumors. They represent a best-case scenario of what Redmi's engineers are aiming for. If Xiaomi can deliver even 80% of what's leaked, the Redmi K90 Ultra could become the most disruptive smartphone of 2026, forcing the entire industry to rethink the balance between battery size and device form factor.
