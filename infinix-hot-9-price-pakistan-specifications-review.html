Infinix Hot 9

December 4, 2025
By: Shedrack
Midnight Black

Price in Pakistan & Overview

The Infinix Hot 9 is a budget smartphone that was originally launched in April 2020 but continues to be a popular choice in Pakistan's affordable segment due to its low price point . According to the latest market data for December 2025, the official price for the base variant (3GB+32GB) in Pakistan is Rs. 17,499 PKR . The higher-end variant (4GB+64GB) is priced at around Rs. 19,999 .

Positioned as a feature-packed entry-level device, the Infinix Hot 9 emphasizes a large display, multiple cameras, and substantial battery capacity . For Pakistani consumers seeking the most basic smartphone functionality at the lowest possible cost, the Hot 9 presents an option, though it is important to note it is a discontinued model with outdated specifications by 2025 standards .

Performance & Hardware

Chipset MediaTek Helio A25 (12nm fabrication)
CPU Configuration Octa-core (4x1.8 GHz Cortex-A53 & 4x1.5 GHz Cortex-A53)
GPU PowerVR GE8320
RAM & Storage Options 2GB/3GB/4GB RAM with 32GB/64GB/128GB internal storage (eMMC 5.1)
Expandable Storage microSDXC (dedicated slot)
Operating System Android 10 with XOS 6.0
Benchmark Scores (3DMark) Sling Shot Extreme (OpenGL ES 3.1): 390; Graphics Test: 1-2 FPS

Performance Analysis: The Helio A25 is an entry-level chipset designed for very basic tasks . User reviews and benchmarks indicate it provides fluid performance for simple operations like calls, messaging, and light browsing, but struggles significantly with multitasking or gaming . The low benchmark graphics scores (1-2 FPS in tests) confirm it is not suitable for modern mobile games .

Camera System - Detailed Review

The quad-camera setup is a marketing highlight, though its real-world performance is limited .

Main Camera (Rear) 13 MP or 16 MP (wide), PDAF, depending on variant
Additional Rear Lenses 2 MP (macro), 2 MP (depth), QVGA low-light sensor
Camera Features Triple-LED flash, panorama, HDR
Video Recording (Rear) 1080p at 30fps (No stabilization)
Selfie Camera (Front) 8 MP, (wide)
Special Feature "360 Flash" using combined front and rear flash

Camera Performance: Reviews note that the camera setup performs below expectations, especially in poorly lit environments . The lack of any image stabilization (OIS or EIS) makes video footage look "very rough" . The additional 2MP macro and depth sensors offer limited utility, and the primary 13MP/16MP sensor is adequate only for basic daylight photography.

Battery & Connectivity

Battery Capacity 5000 mAh non-removable Li-Po battery
Charging Speed Standard 10W charging (No fast charging)
Charging Time Approximately 3 hours for a full charge
Battery Life ~38 hours with moderate use, ~20 hours with heavy use
Network Connectivity 4G LTE (No 5G support)
Audio & Ports 3.5mm headphone jack, mono speaker, microUSB 2.0 (Not USB-C)
Other Connectivity Wi-Fi 5, Bluetooth 5.0, FM Radio, GPS

Battery & Charging Analysis: The 5000mAh battery is the phone's strongest asset, providing excellent endurance that can last up to two days with light use . However, the lack of fast charging and the outdated microUSB port are significant drawbacks in 2025 .

User Reviews & Common Feedback

Aggregating user opinions provides a clear picture of the real-world experience with this older model:

Positive Feedback:

  • Excellent Battery Life: The 5000mAh battery is consistently praised as the best feature, easily lasting 1.5-2 days .
  • Affordable Price Point: At under Rs. 20,000, it is considered a very budget-friendly option for basic needs .
  • Large Display: The 6.6-inch screen is appreciated for media consumption despite its HD+ resolution .

Common Criticisms & Considerations:

  • Outdated Performance: The Helio A25 and low RAM (2GB/3GB) are frequently cited as insufficient for anything beyond basic tasks, with noticeable lag during multitasking .
  • Poor Gaming Performance: The phone struggles with games, with benchmark results showing very low frame rates (1-3 FPS) .
  • Outdated Software & Ports: Shipping with Android 10 (no major update expected) and using microUSB instead of USB-C are seen as major cons in 2025 .
  • Average Camera Quality: The quad-camera setup does not deliver high-quality photos, particularly in low light .
  • Device Status: The phone has been discontinued by the manufacturer, which may affect warranty and parts availability .

Final Verdict: Who Should Consider It?

Consider the Infinix Hot 9 only if:

  • Your absolute maximum budget is Rs. 17,000-20,000 PKR and you need a new phone immediately.
  • Your primary needs are basic calling, texting, WhatsApp, and light web browsing.
  • Exceptional battery life is your top priority over performance, camera quality, or modern features.
  • You are purchasing for an elderly user or as a secondary backup device with minimal requirements.
  • You are aware that you are buying a discontinued 2020 model with outdated software and hardware.

Avoid the Infinix Hot 9 and look for alternatives if:

  • You need smooth performance for social media multitasking, using multiple apps, or any form of gaming.
  • You want a modern smartphone experience with current software (Android 13/14), USB-C, or fast charging.
  • Camera quality for photos and videos is important to you.
  • You can stretch your budget slightly; newer models in the Rs. 25,000-35,000 range (like the Infinix Smart 8 or similar from other brands) offer dramatically better performance and modern features .
  • You prefer to buy a device that is still in production and supported.

Priced at Rs. 17,499 in Pakistan in 2025, the Infinix Hot 9 is a testament to how long a budget phone can remain on the market. It serves a very specific niche: the absolute price-conscious buyer who values battery life above all else and has zero performance expectations. For most users in 2025, even a slightly higher investment will yield a significantly better and more future-proof smartphone experience.
