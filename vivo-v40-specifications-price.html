Vivo V40 - Full Phone Specifications, Price & 2025 Review

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

2025 Price & Market Update

The Vivo V40 was launched globally in the second half of 2024. As of December 2025, it remains a current-generation model in many markets. Its price varies significantly by region and configuration. For reference, its 2024 launch price in Europe was around €400 (~$435), while in India the 8GB/128GB model launched at ₹34,999 (~$420). Prices may have dropped slightly, but it is not considered a discounted "old" phone.

Ganges Blue

Overview & 2025 Perspective

The Vivo V40, released in mid-2024, is a photography-focused mid-range smartphone that continues the V-series tradition of prioritizing camera innovation. In the 2025 landscape, it stands as a compelling option for users who value portrait photography, a sleek design, and robust battery life over absolute raw processing power.

Its headline partnership with ZEISS provides a legitimate professional imaging pedigree, while the large 5500mAh battery in a remarkably slim 7.58mm body addresses key user concerns. While newer chipsets have since been released, the Qualcomm Snapdragon 7 Gen 3 remains a capable and efficient performer for daily tasks and moderate gaming.

Note: Be careful not to confuse the Vivo V40 with the more powerful V40 Pro. The Pro model features a superior MediaTek Dimensity 9200+ chipset and adds a dedicated 2x optical zoom telephoto camera.

Design, Build & Display

Dimensions & Weight 164.2 x 75 x 7.6 mm, 190 g
Build Materials Glass front and back, plastic frame
Durability Rating IP68 & IP69 dust/water resistance (1.5m for 30 mins)
Display Type & Size 6.78-inch AMOLED
Resolution & Refresh Rate 1260 x 2800 pixels (1.5K), 120Hz adaptive refresh rate
Peak Brightness & Protection 4500 nits (peak), Schott Xensation Alpha glass

The V40's design is a major strength. It manages to house a massive 5500mAh battery in a body that's only 7.6mm thick, making it exceptionally sleek for its class. The IP68 & IP69 rating is a premium feature that provides excellent protection against water and dust. The 1.5K AMOLED display is vibrant and smooth, with an incredibly high peak brightness ideal for outdoor use.

Performance, Hardware & Software

Chipset Qualcomm Snapdragon 7 Gen 3 (4nm process)
CPU & GPU Octa-core CPU (up to 2.63 GHz), Adreno 720 GPU
Memory & Storage Options 8GB/12GB RAM with 128GB/256GB/512GB UFS 2.2 storage
Expandable Storage No microSD card slot
Operating System Shipped with Android 14, Funtouch OS 14

The Snapdragon 7 Gen 3 is a balanced mid-range chip. It delivers smooth performance for everyday apps, social media, and popular games like COD Mobile at high settings. Vivo claims an Antutu score of around 830,000. While it won't match 2025's flagship chips, it remains perfectly competent. The lack of storage expansion means choosing the right storage variant at purchase is important.

Camera System: ZEISS Co-Engineered

The camera is the V40's centerpiece, developed in partnership with legendary optics company ZEISS.

Rear Main Camera 50 MP, f/1.9, OIS (Optical Image Stabilization)
Rear Ultra-Wide Camera 50 MP, f/2.0, 119° field of view
Front Camera 50 MP, f/2.0, Autofocus
Video Recording 4K @ 30fps on all cameras
Special Feature Dual-LED "Aura Light" ring flash for portraits
ZEISS Portrait Styles

Includes multiple signature ZEISS lens bokeh effects (Biotar, Planar, etc.) for professional-looking portraits.

Cinematic Portrait Video

Allows focus pulls and offers a 2.39:1 cinema aspect ratio for video.

AI Aura Light

The dual-tone ring flash provides smart, adjustable fill lighting for selfies and rear portraits in low light.

In practice, the main 50MP sensor with OIS captures excellent detail and color, especially in good light. The ZEISS collaboration is most apparent in the portrait mode, which produces natural skin tones and attractive background blur. The powerful 50MP front camera with autofocus is a standout for selfie and video call enthusiasts.

Battery, Connectivity & Final 2025 Verdict

Battery Capacity 5500 mAh (Silicon-Carbon Li-Ion)
Charging Speed 80W wired fast charging
Charging Time ~35 minutes for a full charge (from 20%)
Audio Stereo speakers, No 3.5mm headphone jack
Connectivity 5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.4, NFC (market dependent)
Other Features Under-display fingerprint sensor, AI network optimization

The 5500mAh "BlueVolt" battery is one of the largest in any smartphone and provides exceptional endurance, easily lasting 1.5 to 2 days for most users. Paired with 80W fast charging, you get both longevity and quick top-ups. The connectivity suite is modern and complete, including widespread 5G band support.

Who Should Consider the Vivo V40 in 2025?

  • Portrait Photography Enthusiasts: The ZEISS features and Aura Light offer a unique, pro-level portrait toolkit.
  • Battery Life Prioritizers: Users who want the security of a massive battery that charges incredibly fast.
  • Design-Conscious Buyers: Those who appreciate a slim, sleek, and well-protected (IP68/69) device.
  • Selfie and Vlogging Fans: The high-quality 50MP AF front camera is a major advantage.

Potential Limitations to Consider

  • Processor Choice: The Snapdragon 7 Gen 3 is capable but not a top-tier gaming chip. The competing V40 Pro has a significantly more powerful processor.
  • No Telephoto Lens: It lacks a dedicated zoom camera, relying on digital crop from the main sensor.
  • Regional Variations: Features like NFC and specific 5G bands can vary by market.

Final Verdict: The Vivo V40 remains a highly relevant and specialized mid-range contender in 2025. It doesn't try to win on pure specs but excels in delivering a fantastic photography experience (especially for portraits), class-leading battery life in a sleek body, and premium build quality. If your priorities align with its strengths, it represents excellent value, especially if you can find it at a slightly discounted price compared to its 2024 launch.
