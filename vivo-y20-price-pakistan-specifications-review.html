vivo Y20

Overview & Current Price (2025)

The vivo Y20, originally launched in August 2020, continues to be a staple in Pakistan's budget smartphone market due to its attractive price point and core specifications. As of December 2025, the official price for a new vivo Y20 in Pakistan is approximately Rs. 26,999 for the 4GB RAM and 64GB storage variant. Prices in the used or "without warranty" market can range from Rs. 11,000 to Rs. 20,000, depending on condition.

This phone was designed as an entry-level device focusing on essential features like a large battery and basic performance. While its hardware is dated by 2025 standards, its affordability keeps it relevant for first-time smartphone users or those needing a reliable secondary device.

Detailed Review & Performance in 2025

Design & Display

The vivo Y20 features a dated but functional design with noticeable bezels and a waterdrop notch. The 6.51-inch HD+ IPS LCD display is adequate for basic media consumption but lacks the sharpness and vibrant colors of modern panels. The 60Hz refresh rate feels standard but lags behind today's smoother displays.

Performance & Software

Powered by the Qualcomm Snapdragon 460 chipset and 4GB of RAM, the Y20 handles everyday tasks like calls, messaging, and light social media browsing acceptably. However, users in 2025 report that the phone feels slow, especially with multitasking or newer apps. The outdated Android 10 operating system is a significant drawback, lacking recent security patches and features. Gaming performance is limited to very casual titles; demanding games like PUBG Mobile will struggle.

Camera Quality

The triple-camera setup is basic. The 13MP primary sensor can capture decent shots in good lighting, but the 2MP macro and depth sensors add little practical value. Image processing is often overdone, and low-light performance is poor. The 8MP selfie camera is sufficient for video calls. For the price in 2025, camera performance is a weak point compared to newer budget phones.

Battery Life

This is the Y20's strongest suit. The large 5000mAh battery can easily power the modest hardware for a full day or more of typical use, which remains a key selling point. However, charging via the older microUSB port with 10W/18W fast charging feels slow by modern standards.

Who Should (and Shouldn't) Buy It in 2025?

Consider the vivo Y20 if:

  • Your absolute maximum budget is Rs. 25,000-27,000 for a new phone.
  • You are a very light user who only needs calls, WhatsApp, and YouTube.
  • You prioritize battery life above all else and can compromise on speed and camera.
  • You are buying a secondary or backup phone.

Avoid the vivo Y20 if:

  • You want a smooth, future-proof experience (the outdated OS and chipset will frustrate you).
  • You enjoy mobile gaming or use multiple apps simultaneously.
  • You care about camera quality or want a modern design.
  • You can stretch your budget slightly for a newer model with better specs and software support.

The Verdict: The vivo Y20 is a phone from a different era. While its battery life is commendable and its price is low, its outdated software, sluggish performance by 2025 standards, and mediocre camera make it a hard recommendation. It is only suitable for the most budget-conscious, patient users with minimal expectations. Exploring slightly newer models in the same price range is highly advisable.
