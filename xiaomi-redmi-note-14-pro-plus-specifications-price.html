Xiaomi Redmi Note 14 Pro+ 5G: Full Phone Specifications, Price & Review

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

Latest Price Updates

The Redmi Note 14 Pro+ 5G has seen significant discounts since its launch. Global pricing varies widely by region, with European markets seeing the highest retail prices. Check current deals below before purchasing.

Lavender Purple (Vegan Leather)

Overview & Market Position

The Xiaomi Redmi Note 14 Pro+ 5G, announced on January 10, 2025 and released on January 15, 2025, represents the peak of Redmi's mid-range smartphone ambition. Sitting at the top of the Note 14 series, it brings together a flagship-level 200MP camera system, a stunning 1.5K AMOLED display, and the incredibly fast 120W HyperCharge technology.

Key Consideration: Software Experience

While the hardware impresses, independent reviews note that the Xiaomi HyperOS software experience can be polarizing. The interface includes numerous pre-installed apps and system notifications that some users may find intrusive. This is an important factor to weigh against the phone's strong hardware specifications.

This phone directly competes in the crowded mid-to-high segment against rivals like the Google Pixel 9a, Samsung Galaxy A56 5G, and Nothing Phone 3a Pro. Its combination of premium features like IP68 water resistance and an aluminum frame with a glass or vegan leather back makes it a compelling option for users seeking flagship-like qualities without the flagship price.

Value Proposition & Competition

At its official European price point, the Redmi Note 14 Pro+ faces stiff competition. Reviewers suggest that while it offers excellent hardware—particularly the display and charging speed—voters concerned with software experience might find better value in competitors like the Google Pixel 9a, which offers a cleaner software experience and potentially better camera consistency.

However, when found on sale (discounts of £100/€100 or more are not uncommon), the phone transforms into a much more compelling purchase, where its hardware advantages can be fully appreciated without the software drawbacks feeling as significant.

The design is a standout feature, with reviewers particularly praising the Lavender Purple variant's vegan leather back for its comfortable, grippy texture that resists fingerprints. The phone features a 3D curved screen that fits well in the hand. The inclusion of IP68 rating at this price point is a significant advantage over many competitors and provides peace of mind against accidental spills or rain.

While not a top-tier flagship chip, it strikes a balance between performance and power efficiency for the price. The ample vapor chamber cooling helps maintain performance during longer gaming sessions.

Real-World Camera Performance

The 200MP main camera is the star of the show. In good light, it captures detailed photos with vibrant (sometimes oversaturated) colors suitable for social media. The Optical Image Stabilization (OIS) helps with low-light shots and steady video. The 4x lossless zoom capability, achieved through sensor cropping and AI, is a useful feature.

Notable Limitations: Independent testing reveals significant drop-offs with the auxiliary cameras. The 8MP ultra-wide camera produces noticeably softer images with less detail. The 2MP macro camera is described as having very low quality, suitable only for ideal lighting conditions. For most users, the main camera will be the primary tool.

Video & AI Features

The rear system can record video at up to 4K@30fps with gyro-EIS and OIS stabilization. A suite of AI-powered editing tools like AI Erase Pro (to remove objects) and AI Image Expansion are available, with some features rolling out via software updates. The phone also integrates Google's AI features like Circle to Search and access to the Gemini assistant.

Wired Charging 120W Xiaomi HyperCharge

Audio Stereo speakers with Dolby Atmos, No 3.5mm jack
Connectivity Wi-Fi 6/6E, Bluetooth 5.4, NFC, IR Blaster
Battery Endurance Approximately 26-30 hours of typical use

The battery and charging system is one of the phone's strongest suits. The 5110mAh battery provides excellent all-day endurance, with testers reporting up to 30 hours of typical use. The 120W HyperCharge is exceptionally fast, capable of fully charging the phone in about 25 minutes. A significant point to note is that the 120W power adapter is NOT included in the box in many regions; a USB-C cable and a standard protective case are included instead.

Final Verdict & Who Should Buy It

Choose the Redmi Note 14 Pro+ 5G if:

  • You prioritize charging speed above all else (120W is exceptional).
  • You want a premium-looking and feeling device with IP68 rating at a mid-range price.
  • You value a high-quality, bright, and smooth 1.5K 120Hz display for media consumption.
  • You mostly use the main camera and are satisfied with good, vibrant 200MP photos.
  • You can purchase it on a significant sale, which dramatically improves its value proposition.

Consider Other Options if:

  • A clean, bloatware-free software experience is your top priority.
  • You heavily rely on ultra-wide or macro cameras for your photography.
  • You need wireless charging functionality.
  • You are in the United States and prefer official carrier support and warranties.

The Bottom Line: The Xiaomi Redmi Note 14 Pro+ 5G is a hardware powerhouse with some of the best specs in its class, particularly its display and charging. However, its appeal is heavily dependent on finding a good sale price and being tolerant of Xiaomi's HyperOS software approach. At full price, the competition is fierce, but when discounted, it becomes a very compelling package for the specs-conscious buyer.
