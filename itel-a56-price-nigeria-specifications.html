Itel A56

December 4, 2025
By: Shedrack
Deep Black

Price in Nigeria & Overview

The Itel A56 stands as one of Nigeria's most popular entry-level smartphones, offering essential features at an incredibly affordable price point. As of December 2025, the market price for the Itel A56 in Nigeria ranges between ₦34,000 to ₦40,000 (approximately $45-$53 USD), depending on the retailer and location.

Originally launched in 2020, the Itel A56 continues to be relevant in 2025 due to its massive battery life and basic smartphone functionality that meets the needs of first-time smartphone users and those on a tight budget. For Nigerians looking for a reliable communication device that can last through the day without frequent charging, the A56 presents a compelling value proposition.

Design & Display

Dimensions & Weight 159.7 x 77.7 x 8.9 mm, Weight: 164 grams
Build Materials Plastic body with scratch-resistant glass front
Display Type & Size 6.0-inch IPS LCD capacitive touchscreen
Resolution & Quality 480 x 960 pixels (HD+), 18:9 aspect ratio, ~242 PPI
Screen Features Bezel-less display with notch, 480 nits brightness
Screen-to-Body Ratio Approximately 73.2%

Performance & Hardware

Chipset UniSoC Spreadtrum SC7731E (32nm fabrication)
CPU Quad-core 1.3 GHz Cortex-A7
GPU ARM Mali T820 MP1
RAM 1GB
Internal Storage 16GB
Expandable Storage Yes, microSD card support up to 64GB
Operating System Android 9.0 (Pie) Go Edition

Performance Note: The Android Go Edition is optimized for devices with 1-2GB RAM, featuring lighter versions of Google apps (YouTube Go, Gmail Go, etc.) that consume less storage and data. This makes the phone suitable for basic tasks but limited for heavy multitasking or gaming.

Camera System

Rear Camera Setup Dual: 8MP primary (f/1.8) + 0.3MP depth sensor
Rear Camera Features LED flash, HDR, panorama, digital zoom
Video Recording (Rear) 1080p at 30fps
Front Camera 5MP (f/2.0) with front-facing LED flash
Selfie Features HDR, portrait mode, AI beauty mode
Video Recording (Front) 1080p at 30fps

Camera Performance: The camera performs adequately in good lighting conditions but struggles in low light. The front-facing LED flash is a helpful feature for selfies in dim environments. For its price range, the camera setup is functional for social media and basic photography needs.

Battery & Connectivity

Battery Capacity 4000mAh non-removable Li-Po battery
Battery Life Up to 7 days standby, 30 hours talk time (2G)
Charging Standard charging (no fast charging)
Network Connectivity 2G, 3G, 4G LTE
SIM Hybrid Dual SIM (Nano-SIM)
Other Connectivity Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n, Bluetooth 4.2, GPS, microUSB 2.0, FM Radio

Battery Performance: The 4000mAh battery is the standout feature of the Itel A56. With minimal usage, it can last up to a week, while moderate usage (calls, messaging, light browsing) provides 2-3 days of battery life. This makes it ideal for users with limited access to electricity or frequent travelers.

Additional Features & Security

Security Features Rear-mounted fingerprint sensor, Face Unlock
Sensors Accelerometer, proximity, compass, light sensor
Audio 3.5mm headphone jack, loudspeaker
Pre-installed Utilities Power Master, App Freeze, Phone Manager for optimization
Colors Available Aqua Blue, Champagne Gold, Deep Black

User Experience & Review

Pros:

  • Exceptional Battery Life: The 4000mAh battery is the phone's strongest selling point, easily lasting 2-3 days with moderate use.
  • Affordable Price: At ₦34,000-₦40,000, it's one of the most budget-friendly smartphones in Nigeria.
  • Essential Features Included: Surprisingly includes fingerprint sensor and face unlock at this price point.
  • Decent Display: The 6-inch HD+ screen is adequate for media consumption and browsing.

Cons & Limitations:

  • Limited Performance: Only 1GB RAM restricts multitasking and app usage.
  • Basic Camera Quality: Photos are acceptable in good light but poor in low light.
  • Outdated Android Version: Android 9.0 (released in 2018) lacks latest security updates.
  • No Fast Charging: The large battery takes considerable time to charge fully.

Final Verdict: Who Should Buy It?

Buy the Itel A56 if:

  • You need a basic smartphone for calls, messaging, and light browsing
  • Battery life is your top priority and you need a phone that lasts days
  • You're a first-time smartphone user or buying for elderly family members
  • Your budget is strictly under ₦40,000
  • You primarily use your phone for essential functions rather than gaming or heavy apps

Consider other options if:

  • You need to run multiple apps simultaneously or use demanding applications
  • Camera quality is important to you
  • You want a more modern Android version with better security updates
  • You can stretch your budget to ₦50,000-₦70,000 for significantly better performance

The Itel A56 continues to serve an important niche in the Nigerian smartphone market in 2025. While its specifications appear dated, its combination of essential features, exceptional battery life, and ultra-low price point (₦34,000-₦40,000) ensures it remains a viable option for budget-conscious consumers who prioritize battery longevity over performance.
