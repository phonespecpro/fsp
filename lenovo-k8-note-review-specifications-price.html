Lenovo K8 Note Review - Full Phone Specifications & 2025 Price

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

A Look Back at a 2017 Contender

Originally launched in August 2017, the Lenovo K8 Note was a significant player in the budget segment, praised for bringing stock Android and capable hardware to an affordable price point. This review revisits its specifications and assesses its potential as a legacy device in the 2025 market, where it can occasionally be found at heavily discounted prices.

Venom Black

Overview & 2025 Perspective

The Lenovo K8 Note made waves upon its release by offering a clean stock Android experience—a rarity from Lenovo at the time—paired with a deca-core processor and a dual-camera setup, all at a starting price of around ₹12,999. It was positioned as a direct competitor to popular devices like the Xiaomi Redmi Note 4.

In 2025, the phone is a discontinued legacy model. Its relevance lies as an ultra-budget option for basic tasks or a secondary device. While its original strengths remain, its age comes with significant caveats regarding software support and modern performance expectations.

Design, Build & Display

Build Glass front (Gorilla Glass 3), aluminum back and frame
Dimensions & Weight 154.5 x 75.9 x 8.5 mm, 180 g
Special Feature Splash-resistant coating
Display Size & Type 5.5-inch, IPS LCD
Resolution & Protection Full HD+ (1080x1920 pixels), ~401 PPI, Corning Gorilla Glass 3
Unique Hardware Key Customizable 'Music Key' on the left side

The K8 Note featured a sturdy, all-metal unibody design that felt premium for its price. The 5.5-inch Full HD display was considered vibrant and sharp at the time, though by 2025 standards, the bezels are noticeably large.

Performance, Hardware & Software

Chipset MediaTek MT6797D Helio X23 (20 nm)
CPU & GPU Deca-core (10-core) up to 2.3 GHz, Mali-T880 MP4 GPU
RAM & Storage Options 3GB RAM with 32GB storage or 4GB RAM with 64GB storage
Expandable Storage microSDXC (dedicated slot)
Original OS Android 7.1.1 (Nougat), upgradable to Android 8.0 (Oreo)
Key Software Feature Clean, stock Android user interface (a major selling point)

The Helio X23 deca-core processor and 4GB RAM variant delivered smooth performance for everyday apps and moderate gaming in its prime. However, a common critique was that the device tended to heat up under sustained load. The move to stock Android was universally praised for its cleanliness and speed. Warning for 2025 buyers: The phone is stuck on the outdated and unsupported Android 8.0 (Oreo), posing potential security risks.

Camera System Review

Rear Camera (Dual) 13 MP, f/1.7 (primary) + 5 MP (depth sensor)
Rear Features PDAF, dual-LED dual-tone flash, dedicated depth mode for bokeh
Front Camera 13 MP, f/2.0 with LED flash
Video Recording 1080p @ 30fps (front and rear)

The dual-camera setup was a headline feature. In good light, the primary sensor captured detailed and saturated photos. The depth sensor enabled a background blur (bokeh) effect, though edge detection could be inconsistent. The camera's main weakness was low-light performance, which produced noisy and soft images. The 13MP front camera with an LED flash was a boon for selfie enthusiasts at the time.

Battery, Connectivity & Audio

Battery 4000 mAh, non-removable
Charging 15W wired charging
Network 4G LTE (no 5G support)
Connectivity Wi-Fi 802.11 a/b/g/n/ac, Bluetooth 4.2, microUSB 2.0
Audio 3.5mm headphone jack, Dolby Atmos tuning, mono speaker
Other Features Rear-mounted fingerprint sensor, FM radio

The 4000mAh battery was sizable, though reviews reported average to slightly disappointing battery life, potentially due to the power-hungry 10-core chip. It supports 15W charging via the now-outdated microUSB port. The inclusion of Dolby Atmos enhanced audio quality through headphones, but the single bottom-firing speaker was underwhelming.

2025 Price, Value & Long-Term Verdict

As of December 2025, the Lenovo K8 Note can be found on Indian marketplaces like Flipkart at drastically reduced prices:

  • 3GB/32GB variant: Around ₹6,499
  • 4GB/64GB variant: Around ₹7,799 - ₹8,499
Reasons To Consider in 2025 Major Drawbacks & Warnings
  • Extremely Low Cost: For basic calls, messaging, and light apps.
  • Solid Build Quality: Durable metal body that has aged well physically.
  • Clean Software Experience: Stock Android is still snappy for fundamental tasks.
  • All-Arounder Specs: Has features like a good display, fingerprint sensor, and multi-day battery that are still functional.
  • Outdated & Insecure Software: Stuck on Android 8.0 Oreo with no security updates.
  • Potential Hardware Issues: User reports mention problems like sudden rebooting, Bluetooth faults, and battery degradation requiring costly motherboard replacements.
  • No Modern Connectivity: Lacks 5G, uses old microUSB port, and has a slower Bluetooth standard.
  • Performance Limitations: Will struggle with modern, demanding apps and games.

Final Verdict (2025 Update)

The Lenovo K8 Note is a compromised relic of the 2017 budget wars. It is not recommended as a primary smartphone in 2025 due to its unsupported software and potential for age-related hardware failures.

Consider it ONLY if: You need an absolute bare-minimum backup phone, a device for a child's first phone under strict supervision, or a dedicated media remote. Even then, be aware of the risks. For a similar ultra-low budget in 2025, newer entry-level phones with current software support, while less powerful on paper, are a significantly safer and more reliable choice.
