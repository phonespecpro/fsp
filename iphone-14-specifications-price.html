iPhone 14 - Full Phone Specifications & 2025 Price Review

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

2025 Market Status & Note on Pricing

Launched in September 2022, the iPhone 14 was officially discontinued by Apple following the launch of newer models. As of late 2025, it is no longer sold as new by Apple in most regions. However, it remains widely available through Apple's Certified Refurbished store, major retailers' clearance sales, and the robust used market. All prices discussed are estimates for these channels in USD. It's important to know that U.S. models are eSIM-only, lacking a physical SIM tray.

2025 Overview: The Discontinued Value Contender

The iPhone 14, Apple's mainstream flagship for 2022, represented a focused evolution over the iPhone 13. While its design and core A15 Bionic chip were familiar, it introduced critical new safety features like Emergency SOS via Satellite and Crash Detection.

In the 2025 smartphone landscape, the iPhone 14 occupies a unique niche. Discontinued but still supported with iOS updates, it offers a near-flagship Apple experience at a significantly reduced entry point compared to the latest models. For budget-conscious buyers or those seeking a reliable secondary iPhone, it presents a compelling case based on its balance of performance, features, and price.

Design, Build & Display

Build Materials Glass front and back (Ceramic Shield), aerospace-grade aluminum frame
Dimensions & Weight 146.7 x 71.5 x 7.8 mm, 172 g
Durability Rating IP68 dust/water resistant (up to 6 meters for 30 minutes)
Display Type & Size 6.1-inch Super Retina XDR OLED
Resolution & Brightness 2532 x 1170 pixels (460 ppi), 1200 nits peak HDR brightness
Refresh Rate 60Hz (standard)

The iPhone 14 retains the iconic flat-edge design with a vibrant 6.1-inch OLED display. While it lacks the 120Hz ProMotion refresh rate of the Pro models, the screen is sharp, color-accurate, and gets very bright. The Ceramic Shield front and IP68 rating provide excellent durability for everyday use.

Performance, Hardware & Software

Chipset Apple A15 Bionic (5nm process) - 5-core GPU variant
CPU & GPU Hexa-core CPU (2x3.23 GHz + 4x?), 5-core Apple GPU
RAM 6GB
Storage Options 128GB, 256GB, 512GB (NVMe)
Original / Current OS Shipped with iOS 16. Supports iOS 18 (as of late 2025)
Connectivity (U.S.) 5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3, eSIM only (no physical SIM)

Performance is a strong suit. The A15 Bionic chip, while from 2022, remains exceptionally fast and efficient, handling all daily tasks, intensive apps, and games with ease. The 6GB of RAM (an upgrade from the iPhone 13's 4GB) improves multitasking. Continued support for iOS 18 ensures modern features and security. The shift to eSIM-only in the U.S. is a key detail for travelers or those with specific carrier needs.

Camera System

The dual-camera system sees meaningful upgrades. The main sensor is larger, capturing 49% more light according to Apple, significantly improving low-light performance via the new Photonic Engine computational pipeline. The front camera gains autofocus for sharper selfies and video calls. While it lacks the 48MP sensor and telephoto lens of the Pro models, the camera is highly capable for most users.

Battery, Safety Features & 2025 Price Analysis

Battery Capacity 3,279 mAh (approx.)
Battery Life Apple claims up to 20 hours video playback. Generally provides all-day battery.
Charging Wired (Lightning, up to 20W), MagSafe wireless (up to 15W), Qi wireless (7.5W)
Key Safety Features Emergency SOS via Satellite, Crash Detection
Other Features Face ID, stereo speakers, no 3.5mm headphone jack

Battery life is solid for a full day. The standout additions are the life-saving safety features: Emergency SOS via Satellite allows contacting emergency services without cellular coverage, and Crash Detection can automatically call for help after a severe car accident.

Final 2025 Verdict: Who Should Buy It?

The Bottom Line

The iPhone 14 in 2025 is a premium, polished, and capable smartphone available at a mid-range price. It lacks the cutting-edge features of the latest Pros (Dynamic Island, 120Hz, telephoto camera) but delivers 95% of the core iPhone experience for significantly less money.

Buy the iPhone 14 in 2025 if:

  • You want a near-flagship Apple experience without paying flagship 2025 prices.
  • Software support and longevity are priorities (it runs iOS 18).
  • You value the added safety net of Satellite SOS and Crash Detection.
  • You're comfortable buying from Apple Refurbished or reputable used/clearance channels.

Consider a newer or different model if:

  • You must have a physical SIM tray (U.S. models are eSIM-only).
  • A 120Hz ProMotion display, Dynamic Island, or a dedicated telephoto camera are important to you.
  • You prefer (or your budget allows for) a brand-new, current-generation device.
  • You want the absolute best battery life or camera system Apple offers.

Conclusion: The iPhone 14 has matured into one of the best-value propositions in the Apple ecosystem. For the savvy shopper who doesn't need the very latest specs, it offers exceptional build quality, reliable performance, modern software, and unique safety features at an attractive price point. It's a smart buy that continues to deliver excellent value years after its release.
