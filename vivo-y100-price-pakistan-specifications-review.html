Vivo Y100

December 4, 2025
By: Shedrack
Crystal Black

Price in Pakistan & Overview

The Vivo Y100 has established itself as a strong contender in Pakistan's competitive mid-range smartphone segment. As of December 2025, the official price for the 8GB+128GB variant in Pakistan is Rs. 56,999, with the higher-end 8GB+256GB model priced at approximately Rs. 61,999 .

Launched globally in early 2024, the Vivo Y100 focuses on delivering premium features typically found in more expensive devices, including a vibrant AMOLED display, exceptionally fast charging, and a capable camera system . For Pakistani consumers seeking a balanced smartphone with standout charging speeds and display quality around the Rs. 60,000 mark, the Y100 presents a compelling option .

Design & Display

Dimensions & Weight 163.2 x 75.8 x 7.8 mm, 186 grams
Build Materials Plastic frame, plastic back, glass front (varies by region)
Display Type & Size 6.67-inch AMOLED capacitive touchscreen
Resolution & Refresh Rate FHD+ (1080 x 2400 pixels), 120Hz refresh rate
Peak Brightness 1800 nits (peak) - excellent for outdoor visibility
Protection & Rating IP54 dust and splash resistant

Performance & Hardware

Chipset Qualcomm Snapdragon 685 (6nm fabrication)
CPU Configuration Octa-core (4x2.8 GHz Cortex-A73 + 4x1.9 GHz Cortex-A53)
GPU Adreno 610
RAM & Storage Options 8GB RAM (with 8GB extended/virtual RAM) + 128GB/256GB UFS 2.2 storage
Expandable Storage microSDXC (uses shared SIM slot)
Operating System Android 14 with Funtouch OS 14

Performance Note: The Snapdragon 685 is a capable 4G chipset designed for efficient everyday performance rather than heavy gaming. Benchmark reviews indicate it handles daily tasks smoothly but may struggle with graphically intensive games . For typical use—social media, browsing, streaming—it provides a lag-free experience.

Camera System - Detailed Review

The camera setup on the Y100 is designed for reliable everyday photography with some smart enhancements.

Main Camera (Rear) 50 MP, f/1.8, wide sensor, PDAF
Secondary Camera (Rear) 2 MP depth sensor
Additional Sensor Flicker sensor (helps with artificial lighting)
Camera Features LED flash, HDR, panorama
Video Recording (Rear) 1080p@30fps (No 4K recording)
Selfie Camera (Front) 8 MP, f/2.0, wide

Camera Performance: The 50MP primary sensor captures good detail and color in daylight conditions . The lack of optical image stabilization (OIS) and an ultrawide lens are notable omissions at this price. Low-light performance is average, and video recording is capped at 1080p, which may disappoint users expecting 4K .

Battery, Charging & Connectivity

Battery Capacity 5000 mAh non-removable Li-Po battery
Wired Charging Speed 80W fast charging (advertised: 1-80% in 30 mins) - a standout feature
Battery Life Easily lasts 1.5-2 days with moderate use
Network Connectivity 4G LTE (No 5G support in this variant)
Audio Stereo speakers, No 3.5mm headphone jack
Other Connectivity Wi-Fi 5, Bluetooth 5.0, GPS, USB Type-C 2.0, OTG

Battery & Charging Analysis: The combination of a 5000mAh battery and 80W charging is arguably the phone's greatest strength. You get exceptional battery life for all-day (and more) use, coupled with incredibly quick top-ups . The absence of a headphone jack has been criticized by some users .

User Reviews & Common Feedback

Aggregating opinions from various users and reviewers provides a balanced view:

Positive Feedback:

  • Excellent Display: The 120Hz AMOLED screen with 1800 nits brightness receives consistent praise for its quality .
  • Outstanding Charging & Battery: The 80W charging speed and 2-day battery life are frequently highlighted as best-in-class features .
  • Premium Design & Build: Many users appreciate the phone's sleek look and feel, noting its resemblance to more expensive models .

Common Criticisms & Considerations:

  • Average Performance for Gaming: The Snapdragon 685 is not designed for heavy gaming, leading to mediocre benchmark scores and gameplay on lower settings for demanding titles .
  • No 5G Support: In a market increasingly moving toward 5G, its 4G-only connectivity is seen as a limitation for future-proofing .
  • Mixed Camera Reviews: While decent in good light, the camera system, especially the lack of 4K video and ultrawide lens, is considered a weak point by enthusiasts .

Final Verdict: Who Should Buy It?

Buy the Vivo Y100 if:

  • You prioritize blazing-fast charging (80W) and all-day-plus battery life above all else.
  • You value a vibrant, smooth 120Hz AMOLED display for media consumption.
  • Your smartphone usage revolves around social media, calls, messaging, and streaming, not heavy gaming.
  • You want a premium-looking design with features like an under-display fingerprint sensor.
  • 5G connectivity is not a current requirement for you.

Consider other options if:

  • Mobile gaming performance is a top priority for you.
  • You need 5G connectivity for future network upgrades.
  • You are a photography enthusiast who requires versatile lenses (ultrawide, telephoto) or 4K video recording.
  • The absence of a 3.5mm headphone jack is a deal-breaker.

Priced at Rs. 56,999 in Pakistan, the Vivo Y100 excels as a well-rounded daily driver that makes significant compromises in peak performance and camera versatility to deliver exceptional battery life, charging speed, and display quality. It's an ideal choice for the practical user, not the power user .
