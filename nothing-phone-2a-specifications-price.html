Nothing Phone (2a) - Full Phone Specifications & 2025 Price Review

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

US Market Note (December 2025): Official availability of the Phone (2a) in the United States remains limited. It is typically accessed through Nothing's developer program and is not widely available through standard carriers or major retailers. This review assesses its global specifications and market position.

Black

Overview & 2025 Perspective

Launched in March 2024, the Nothing Phone (2a) was Nothing's strategic move into the more affordable mid-range segment, succeeding its higher-end siblings, the Phone (1) and Phone (2). Its goal was to deliver the brand's signature transparent design language and unique user experience at a significantly lower price point.

As of December 2025, the Phone (2a) occupies a distinct niche. It appeals to users who want a smartphone with personality and clever software features, without paying flagship prices. While it makes some expected compromises for its cost, it retains core Nothing DNA, including the iconic (though simplified) Glyph Interface on the back.

The Design & Glyph Interface

The Phone (2a)'s design is its most recognizable feature, though it represents a simplification from the more elaborate Phone (2).

Transparent Aesthetic: The back features a clear polycarbonate plastic panel revealing internal components and three LED light strips, inspired by subway maps.
Simplified Glyphs: Instead of the complex array on the Phone (2), the (2a) has three light strips with 26 addressable zones for notifications, a camera fill light, and other visual cues.
Build & Protection: It features a Gorilla Glass 5 front, a plastic frame and back, and an IP54 rating for protection against dust and water splashes. The phone is notably lightweight at 190g.

Display

Size & Type 6.7-inch AMOLED
Resolution & Refresh Rate 1080 x 2412 pixels (FHD+), 120Hz adaptive refresh rate
Brightness & Features 1300 nits (peak), HDR10+, 2160Hz PWM dimming
Protection Corning Gorilla Glass 5

The 6.7-inch AMOLED is a highlight, offering vibrant colors, deep blacks, and a smooth 120Hz refresh rate for the price. The high peak brightness ensures good outdoor visibility.

Performance & Software

Chipset MediaTek Dimensity 7200 Pro (4nm) - co-developed with Nothing
Memory & Storage 8GB/128GB, 8GB/256GB, 12GB/256GB (UFS 2.2). No microSD slot
Operating System (2025) Currently runs Nothing OS 3.0 based on Android 15
Software Support 3 years of Android OS upgrades, 4 years of security updates

The custom MediaTek Dimensity 7200 Pro provides capable performance for everyday tasks and even handles demanding games like Genshin Impact reasonably well. Reviews noted smooth app switching and no significant lag in daily use. The clean, bloatware-free Nothing OS with its distinct monochrome aesthetic and useful Glyph integrations is a major part of the user experience.

Camera System

Main Camera 50 MP, f/1.9, Samsung GN9 sensor, OIS (Optical Image Stabilization)
Ultra-Wide Camera 50 MP, f/2.2, 114° field of view, Samsung JN1 sensor
Front Camera 32 MP, f/2.2
Video Recording 4K @ 30fps (rear), 1080p @ 60fps (front)

The dual 50MP camera setup is impressive for the price range. The main sensor with OIS captures detailed photos with good dynamic range, while the ultra-wide offers a useful perspective. PCMag's review praised the camera's performance and effective video stabilization.

Battery, Audio & Connectivity

Battery & Charging 5000 mAh, 45W wired fast charging (0-50% in ~23 mins)
Battery Life (Tested) ~11 hours 45 minutes of video playback
Audio Stereo speakers, No 3.5mm headphone jack
Connectivity (US Limitation) 5G, Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.3, NFC. Note: 5G support in the US is largely limited to T-Mobile's network
Other Under-display optical fingerprint sensor, face unlock

The large 5000mAh battery provides solid all-day endurance. The 45W charging is fast, though a charger is not included in the box. The major caveat for US users is the limited 5G band support, which can affect cellular speeds and connectivity depending on your carrier.

2025 Price & Final Verdict

The Nothing Phone (2a) was launched at an aggressive price to compete in the crowded mid-range market. As of December 2025, its price has seen some fluctuation but remains in the budget-to-mid-range category.

Original Launch Price (March 2024): Started at approximately $349 for the base model.

2025 Market Price (Estimated): Prices vary by region and retailer. In the US, the 12GB/256GB model is often listed around $389. It's advisable to check current listings on Amazon or other electronics retailers for the most accurate pricing.

Who Should Consider the Nothing Phone (2a) in 2025?

  • Design-Forward Buyers: If you want a phone that stands out from generic black rectangles and appreciate the transparent aesthetic/Glyph lights.
  • Software Experience Seekers: Users who value a clean, thoughtful Android skin (Nothing OS) with unique features.
  • Value-Conscious Photographers: Those seeking capable dual 50MP cameras without a flagship price tag.
  • Non-US Buyers / T-Mobile Users: The phone is a much more practical choice in markets where it has full carrier support.

Who Might Want to Look Elsewhere?

  • US Buyers on Verizon/AT&T: Due to very limited 5G support on these networks.
  • Durability Prioritizers: The IP54 rating is basic; if you need full water resistance (IP67/68), other options exist.
  • Wireless Charging Fans: The Phone (2a) does not support wireless charging.
  • Those Needing Wide US Availability: It's not easily purchased through standard US channels.

Final Verdict: The Nothing Phone (2a) is a successful and charismatic budget-minded offering. It makes smart compromises to hit its price target, most notably in network compatibility for the US and a simplified Glyph design, while retaining excellent core features like its display, capable processor, and unique software. In 2025, it remains a compelling choice for the right buyer—particularly those outside the US or on T-Mobile who want a distinctive mid-range device with few real performance shortcomings.
