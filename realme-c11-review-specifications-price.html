Realme C11 - Full Phone Specifications & 2025 Price Review

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

Note on 2025 Pricing: The Realme C11 was originally launched in July 2020 at a price of about $99 / €90 . As a discontinued model, its current 2025 price is highly variable and dependent on local retailers, refurbishers, and regional stock. The figures below are estimates based on recent listings and currency conversions from original launch prices.

Mint Green

Overview & 2025 Perspective

Launched in mid-2020, the Realme C11 was Realme's answer for the most budget-conscious smartphone buyer. Its mission was clear: deliver the absolute essentials—a large screen, a massive battery, and a functional camera—at the lowest possible price point, under $100.

In 2025, this phone exists in the second-hand and clearance market. It represents the extreme end of "budget," a device for users whose primary needs are calling, messaging, and media consumption, with battery life being the non-negotiable king. For those with minimal needs and even more minimal budgets, it can still be a consideration, albeit with significant compromises by modern standards.

Realme C11: At a Glance

  • Launch Price (2020):~$99 / €90
  • Est. 2025 Price (Refurbished/New Old Stock):~$65 - $85
  • Battery & Charging:5000mAh, 10W wired
  • Display:6.5" HD+ (720x1600) IPS LCD
  • Processor:MediaTek Helio G35 (12nm)
  • RAM & Storage:2GB/3GB RAM, 32GB Storage
  • Main Camera:13MP (wide) + 2MP (depth)
  • Front Camera:5MP
  • Software (Original):Android 10, Realme UI

Design, Build & Display

Dimensions & Weight 164.4 x 75.9 x 9.1 mm, 196 g
Build Materials Polycarbonate (plastic) body
Design Feature Textured geometric pattern on back to resist fingerprints
Display Size & Type 6.5-inch IPS LCD
Resolution & Notch HD+ (720 x 1600 pixels), Mini-drop (waterdrop) notch
Screen-to-Body Ratio ~88.7%

For its price, the C11's design was a highlight. The polycarbonate back features a unique textured pattern with hundreds of small curves intended to hide smudges, giving it a more interesting look than typical glossy plastic. The 6.5-inch HD+ display is large and adequate for basic videos and browsing, though its 720p resolution is noticeably less sharp than 1080p panels.

Performance, Hardware & Software

Chipset MediaTek Helio G35 (12nm process)
CPU & GPU Octa-core CPU (4x2.3 GHz + 4x1.8 GHz), PowerVR GE8320 GPU
Memory Options 2GB or 3GB of RAM with 32GB of eMMC 5.1 storage
Expandable Storage microSDXC dedicated slot (up to 256GB)
Original Operating System Android 10 with Realme UI

Performance is the phone's biggest compromise. The Helio G35 and 2GB of RAM are strictly for light-duty tasks—social media, messaging, casual browsing. Multitasking is limited, and while Realme marketed the G35 for gaming, reviews found it struggles with heavier games like PUBG Mobile, with significant lag. The software experience is hampered by pre-installed bloatware. Critical 2025 Note: The phone is stuck on the outdated and unsupported Android 10, a major security and compatibility concern.

Camera System

Rear Camera Setup 13 MP, f/2.2 (primary) + 2 MP, f/2.4 (depth sensor)
Rear Camera Features PDAF, LED flash, portrait mode (via depth sensor)
Front Camera 5 MP, f/2.4
Video Recording 1080p at 30fps (both front and rear)

The camera system is basic. In good daylight, the 13MP primary sensor can capture decent, shareable photos with satisfactory detail. The 2MP depth sensor enables a background blur effect in portraits. The camera's Achilles' heel is low-light performance, which produces noisy, soft images. The 5MP front camera is sufficient for video calls.

Battery, Connectivity & Final 2025 Verdict

Battery Capacity 5000 mAh (non-removable)
Charging 10W wired charging. Supports reverse charging via OTG cable
Battery Life Claim Up to 40 days standby, ~31 hours of call time*
Connectivity 4G LTE, Wi-Fi 802.11 b/g/n, Bluetooth 5.0, microUSB 2.0 port
Security Face Unlock only (No fingerprint sensor)
Special Slot Dedicated 3-card slot (2x Nano-SIM + 1x microSD)

The 5000mAh battery is the star of the show. Even with moderate use, it can easily last two full days, with some users reporting much more. However, charging is slow (10W) via an outdated microUSB port. The absence of a fingerprint sensor is a notable omission, even for a budget phone.

Who Might Still Consider It in 2025?

  • Absolute Budget-First Buyers: If your maximum budget is ~$70 and you need a new or like-new device for basic communication.
  • Battery-Life Obsessed Users: As a dedicated backup phone for travel or emergencies where charging is uncertain.
  • First-Time Smartphone Users (e.g., Seniors/Kids): For learning the basics without risk or investment.

Significant Drawbacks & Warnings

  • Outdated & Insecure Software: No official updates since Android 10.
  • Very Limited Performance: 2GB RAM is insufficient for modern apps; expect slowdowns.
  • Poor Longevity: User reviews from 2020-2021 commonly report the phone becoming slow and "hanging" after several months of use.
  • No Warranty: As a discontinued model, manufacturer warranty is unlikely.

Final Verdict: The Realme C11 is a testament to how far the budget segment has come. While its mammoth battery remains impressive, its aged software, weak performance, and lack of support make it a very hard recommendation in 2025. It should only be considered as an absolute last resort for a primary phone, or as a specialized tool (like a dedicated media or backup device) where its shortcomings can be mitigated.
