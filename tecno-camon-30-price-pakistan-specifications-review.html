Price in Pakistan & Overview

The TECNO Camon 30 is positioned as a camera-centric mid-range smartphone that offers remarkable value in the Pakistani market. As of December 2025, the official price for the base variant (8GB+256GB) is Rs. 49,999 PKR (approximately $153 USD) in Pakistan. This price point makes it a compelling competitor against offerings from Samsung, Xiaomi, and other Chinese brands in the same segment.

Launched globally in April 2024, the phone focuses on delivering a premium camera experience, fast charging, and a smooth displayâ€”features typically found in more expensive devices. For users in Pakistan looking for a capable daily driver with excellent photography features without stretching their budget, the Camon 30 presents a strong case.

Design & Display

Dimensions & Weight 165.3 x 75.3 x 7.7 mm (Glass) / 7.82 mm (Vegan Leather). Weight not specified.
Build Materials Glass front, glass back or silicone polymer back (eco leather option). Offers a premium feel in hand.
Display Type & Size 6.78-inch AMOLED capacitive touchscreen.
Resolution & Quality FHD+ (1080 x 2436 pixels), ~393 PPI pixel density.
Refresh Rate 120Hz refresh rate for smooth scrolling and animations.
Additional Features Always-on display functionality.

Performance & Hardware

Chipset MediaTek Helio G99 Ultimate (6nm fabrication). A capable 4G chipset for gaming and multitasking.
CPU Octa-core (2x2.2 GHz Cortex-A76 & 6x2.0 GHz Cortex-A55).
GPU Mali-G57 MC2.
RAM & Storage Variants: 8GB+256GB and 12GB+256GB. Supports extended RAM technology (e.g., 8GB+8GB).
Expandable Storage Yes, dedicated microSDXC slot.
Operating System Android 14 with HIOS 14.
Benchmark Performance UL benchmarks show a PCMark Work 3.0 score of 11310 and a Work 3.0 Battery life of over 13 hours.

Camera System - Detailed Review

The camera is the centerpiece of the Camon 30's marketing, and it largely delivers for its price.

Main Camera (Rear) 50 MP, f/1.9, wide sensor (1/1.57"), PDAF, Optical Image Stabilization (OIS). OIS is a standout feature in this price range, aiding in steady photos and videos.
Secondary Camera (Rear) 2 MP depth sensor.
Camera Features Dual-LED flash, HDR, panorama, dedicated light sensor.
Video Recording (Rear) 1440p@30fps, 1080p@30/60fps.
Selfie Camera (Front) 50 MP, wide, with Autofocus (AF) and a Dual-LED dual-tone flash. The front flash is a bonus for low-light selfies.
Video Recording (Front) 1440p@30fps, 1080p@30/60fps.

User Feedback: Most users praise the camera's performance in daylight and portrait mode. However, note that some online debates question the implementation of OIS, with a few users claiming it behaves more like EIS (Electronic Stabilization). The 50MP front camera with flash is widely appreciated.

Battery, Connectivity & Audio

Battery Capacity 5000 mAh, non-removable Li-Po battery.
Charging Speed 70W wired fast charging. Advertised to go from 0-100% in 45 minutes. This is one of its key selling points.
Network Connectivity 4G LTE (No 5G support). Supports 2G and 3G bands.
Audio Dual stereo speakers with Dolby Atmos support. Includes a 3.5mm headphone jack.
Other Connectivity Wi-Fi 5, Bluetooth 5.3, NFC, Infrared port, FM Radio, USB Type-C 2.0. The IR blaster is a handy feature for controlling appliances.
Sensors Under-display optical fingerprint sensor, accelerometer, gyro, proximity, compass.

User Reviews & Reported Issues

Based on aggregated user opinions from forums and review platforms:

Positive Feedback:

  • Value for Money: Frequently cited as the best phone in its price range (around $190 internationally), especially for the charging speed and camera.
  • Fast Charging: Users are highly satisfied with the 70W speed, reporting a full charge in under 50 minutes.
  • Overall Performance: Most users report smooth performance for daily tasks, social media, and moderate gaming.

Reported Issues & Considerations:

  • Fingerprint Sensor Glitch: A number of users have reported a significant issue where the phone unlocks with unregistered fingerprints. The suggested fix is to delete and re-register fingerprints.
  • No 5G: For future-proofing, the lack of 5G support is a noted drawback.
  • Software & Bloatware: HIOS custom interface may include some bloatware, which is common for the brand.

Final Verdict: Who Should Buy It?

Buy the TECNO Camon 30 if:

  • Your priority is a great camera with OIS at a mid-range budget.
  • You want extremely fast charging (70W) and good battery life.
  • You value a smooth 120Hz AMOLED display and a premium build.
  • You are a content creator or selfie enthusiast (thanks to the 50MP front camera with flash).
  • 5G connectivity is not immediately necessary for you.

Consider other options if:

  • 5G is a must-have for future-proofing your purchase.
  • You prefer a clean, stock Android experience with minimal bloatware.
  • You need the absolute best low-light camera performance or an ultrawide lens (the Camon 30 lacks an ultrawide sensor).

At Rs. 49,999 in Pakistan, the TECNO Camon 30 successfully carves a niche as a feature-packed, camera-focused smartphone that punches above its weight, particularly in charging speed and display quality.
