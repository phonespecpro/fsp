iPhone 11 - Full Phone Specifications & 2025 Price Review

December 2, 2025
By: Shedrack

A Legacy Device with Modern Relevance

The iPhone 11, released in September 2019 and officially discontinued by Apple in September 2022, has carved out a legendary status as one of the best-selling smartphones of all time (selling approximately 159.2 million units as of March 2022). In 2025, it lives on through the refurbished and used market, offering an exceptional value proposition for budget-conscious buyers and those seeking a reliable entry into the Apple ecosystem.

Key 2025 Update: Impressively, the iPhone 11 is still supported with the latest software. It originally launched with iOS 13 and can currently run iOS 18 (as of late 2025), ensuring access to modern apps and critical security updates.

Black

2025 Overview: Why the iPhone 11 Endures

When Apple launched the iPhone 11 in 2019, it was celebrated as the "affordable" flagship, succeeding the popular iPhone XR. Its mission was to deliver the core iPhone experience—powerful performance, a great camera, and all-day battery life—at a more accessible price point. It achieved this so successfully that it became a sales phenomenon.

Fast forward to 2025. The phone is no longer sold as new by Apple, but its legacy is stronger than ever in the secondary market. The combination of its still-capable A13 Bionic chip, continued iOS support, and a significant price drop makes it a uniquely compelling option. It's a testament to Apple's build quality and software longevity that a six-year-old design remains a practical and smart choice for millions.

Design, Build & Display

Build Materials Glass front and back (Corning-made), aerospace-grade aluminum frame
Dimensions & Weight 150.9 x 75.7 x 8.3 mm, 194 g (6.84 oz)
Durability Rating IP68 dust/water resistant (up to 2 meters for 30 minutes)
Display Type & Size 6.1-inch Liquid Retina IPS LCD
Resolution & Technology 1792 x 828 pixels (326 ppi), True Tone, Wide Color (P3)
Peak Brightness 625 nits (typical)

The iPhone 11's design is classic and durable. The IP68 rating provides excellent peace of mind against accidental spills or rain. The 6.1-inch Liquid Retina display, while not an OLED, offers excellent color accuracy and brightness for daily use. The iconic notch houses the TrueDepth camera system for Face ID.

Performance, Hardware & Software

Chipset Apple A13 Bionic (7nm+ process)
CPU & GPU Hexa-core CPU (2x2.65 GHz + 4x1.8 GHz), Apple 4-core GPU
RAM 4GB
Storage Options 64GB, 128GB, 256GB (NVMe)
Original / Current OS Shipped with iOS 13. Currently supports iOS 18 (as of late 2025)
Biometric Security Face ID (via TrueDepth camera system)

This is where the iPhone 11 truly shines for a 2025 buyer. The A13 Bionic chip remains surprisingly potent. While it won't match the latest A19 or A18 chips, it handles everyday tasks—social media, web browsing, streaming, and even moderate gaming—with smooth fluency. The 4GB of RAM is adequate for iOS's efficient memory management.

The most significant advantage is software support. The fact that a phone launched in 2019 can run iOS 18 in 2025 is extraordinary and a key reason for its longevity. This ensures app compatibility, new features, and vital security updates.

Camera System

Rear Wide Camera 12 MP, f/1.8, 26mm, PDAF, OIS (Optical Image Stabilization)
Rear Ultra-Wide Camera 12 MP, f/2.4, 13mm, 120° field of view
Front TrueDepth Camera 12 MP, f/2.2, 23mm
Key Photo Features Night Mode, Portrait Mode with Depth Control, Smart HDR, Deep Fusion
Video Recording 4K at 24/25/30/60fps, 1080p slow-mo up to 240fps

The dual-camera system was a major upgrade from the single-lens iPhone XR. The addition of the ultra-wide lens opened up creative possibilities for landscapes and group shots. Night Mode, introduced with the iPhone 11, was a game-changer for low-light photography and remains effective. For most users, the camera system is more than capable of capturing excellent photos and videos for social media and memories.

Battery, Connectivity & 2025 Price Analysis

Battery Capacity 3110 mAh, non-removable
Battery Life (Apple Claim) Up to 17 hours of video playback
Charging Wired (Lightning, fast-charge capable), Qi wireless charging
Network Connectivity 4G LTE (Gigabit-class). No 5G support
Other Connectivity Wi-Fi 6, Bluetooth 5.0, NFC (for Apple Pay), U1 Ultra Wideband chip
Audio Stereo speakers, No 3.5mm headphone jack

The battery life was one of the iPhone 11's praised features at launch, and with typical daily use, it can still comfortably last a full day in 2025, though battery health on used units will vary. The most notable connectivity limitation in 2025 is the lack of 5G, which means you're limited to 4G LTE speeds. For many users and in many areas, this is still perfectly acceptable.

Final 2025 Verdict: Buy or Pass?

The iPhone 11 in 2025 is a fascinating case study in value and longevity. It is not a cutting-edge device, but it offers a balanced, reliable, and polished smartphone experience at a fraction of the cost of a new iPhone.

Consider the iPhone 11 if:

  • Your budget is strict (under $200): It offers the best blend of iOS experience and performance in this price range.
  • You want a reliable secondary/backup iPhone: It's perfect for a work phone, a teen's first phone, or a spare device.
  • You prioritize software updates: Support for iOS 18 is a massive advantage over Android phones from 2019.
  • You are new to iOS: It's a low-risk, low-cost way to try the Apple ecosystem.

Look for a newer model if:

  • 5G connectivity is essential for you.
  • You need the absolute best camera, a modern design (Dynamic Island), or a brighter OLED display.
  • You are a heavy mobile gamer or power user who demands the latest processor.
  • You prefer to buy a brand-new device with a full manufacturer warranty.

The Bottom Line: The iPhone 11 is a legacy champion that still delivers. For its current market price, it provides exceptional value, a smooth software experience, and a capable all-rounder feature set. If your needs are moderate and your budget is tight, the iPhone 11 remains one of the smartest smartphone purchases you can make in 2025.
